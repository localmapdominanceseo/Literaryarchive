The Curious Case of Benjamin Button

F. Scott Fitzgerald · Written 1922, Published 1922 · ~12,000 words

Published in 1922 in Collier's Weekly, 'The Curious Case of Benjamin Button' tells the story of a man born as a seventy-year-old and who ages in reverse, growing younger throughout his life until he disappears as an infant.

Quick Summary

'The Curious Case of Benjamin Button' is a story by F. Scott Fitzgerald, published in 1922. Benjamin Button is born in 1860 as a seventy-year-old man. He ages in reverse — growing younger as the years pass. He marries, has a son, goes to war as a man in his forties who appears to be in his twenties, attends Harvard at fifty. By the end he is an infant and then nothing.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

A comic fable told in third-person narration with deadpan absurdist logic — the premise is accepted as literal and its consequences followed through.

Themes

A satire on American ideas of progress and youth — Benjamin's reverse aging means he is always out of step, always at odds with social expectation.

Historical Context

Published in Collier's Weekly, May 1922. Collected in Tales of the Jazz Age (1922).

Extract

As long ago as 1860 it was the proper thing to be born at home. At present, so I am told, the high gods of medicine have decreed that the first cries of the young shall be uttered upon the anesthetic air of a hospital, preferably a fashionable one. So young Mr. and Mrs. Roger Button were fifty years ahead of style when they decided, one day in the summer of 1860, that their first baby should be born in a hospital.

Public domain. First published in Collier's Weekly, May 27, 1922.

About F. Scott Fitzgerald

Francis Scott Key Fitzgerald was born on September 24, 1896, in St. Paul, Minnesota. The Great Gatsby (1925) is his masterpiece — the definitive novel of the American Dream's promise and betrayal. His short stories, collected in Flappers and Philosophers and Tales of the Jazz Age, are equally important to the period. He died in Hollywood in 1940.
