The Most Dangerous Game

Richard Connell · Written 1924, Published 1924 · ~8,000 words

Published in 1924 in Collier's Weekly, 'The Most Dangerous Game' is Richard Connell's classic adventure story. Rainsford, a celebrated big-game hunter, falls overboard at night and swims to an island. There he meets General Zaroff, a Russian aristocrat who has grown bored hunting animals — and now hunts men. Rainsford becomes his next prey.

Quick Summary

'The Most Dangerous Game' is a story by Richard Connell, published in 1924. Big-game hunter Rainsford falls off a yacht and swims to an island owned by General Zaroff, who explains he has grown bored hunting animals and now hunts men. Zaroff hunts Rainsford across the island for three days. Rainsford survives and confronts Zaroff in his bedroom. The story ends with Rainsford sleeping in Zaroff's bed.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

Third-person adventure narration, fast-paced and suspenseful, built around the cat-and-mouse structure of the hunt.

Themes

The story reverses the hunter-prey relationship and asks what separates human beings from animals. Zaroff's argument is that the intelligent man is simply a more interesting quarry. Rainsford's survival requires him to think like a hunter — and by the end he has become one who hunts humans.

Historical Context

Published in Collier's Weekly in 1924. One of the most widely anthologized adventure stories in American literature, often taught in secondary schools.

Key Passage

'I am still a beast at bay,' said Rainsford, in a low, hoarse voice. 'Get ready, General Zaroff.'

Extract

'Off there to the right — somewhere — is a large island,' said Whitney. 'It's rather a mystery — '

'What island is it?' Rainsford asked.

'The old charts call it Ship-Trap Island,' Whitney replied.' A suggestive name, isn't it? Sailors have a curious dread of the place. I don't know why. Some superstition — '

'Can't see it,' remarked Rainsford, trying to peer through the dank tropical night that was palpable as it pressed its thick, warm blackness in upon the yacht.

'You've good eyes,' said Whitney, with a laugh,' and I've seen you pick off a moose moving through the woods at four hundred yards, but even you can't see four miles or so through a moonless Caribbean night.'

Public domain. First published in Collier's Weekly, January 19, 1924.

About Richard Connell

Richard Connell was born on October 17, 1893, in Poughkeepsie, New York. He is remembered primarily for one story: 'The Most Dangerous Game' (1924), which became one of the most widely anthologized and adapted adventure stories of the twentieth century. He died in Beverly Hills, California, in 1949.
