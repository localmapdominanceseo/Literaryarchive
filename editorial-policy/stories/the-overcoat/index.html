The Overcoat

Nikolai Gogol · Written 1842, Published 1842 · ~9,000 words

Published in 1842, 'The Overcoat' is Nikolai Gogol's most celebrated story. Akaky Akakievich Bashmachkin, a lowly copying clerk in St. Petersburg, saves for months to buy a new overcoat. The night he first wears it, it is stolen. He appeals to the Important Personage for help and is dismissed with a reprimand. He falls ill and dies. His ghost haunts the city, stealing overcoats.

Quick Summary

'The Overcoat' is a story by Nikolai Gogol, published in 1842. Akaky Akakievich, a poor copying clerk, scrimps and saves to buy a new overcoat. His colleagues mock his joy. The first night he wears it, it is stolen. He appeals to an Important Personage who shouts at him. He dies of a fever. His ghost haunts St. Petersburg, tearing overcoats from passersby — until it tears the coat from the Important Personage himself.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

Third-person narration with Gogol's characteristic mixture of comedy and pathos — treating a tragic subject with seemingly comic detachment.

Themes

The story attacks the dehumanization of bureaucracy — Akaky is a nothing in the government hierarchy, defined only by his function. His overcoat becomes his only source of identity and dignity. His death moves no one above him.

Historical Context

Published 1842. Dostoevsky's famous remark — 'We all came out from under Gogol's Overcoat' — captures its foundational importance for Russian literature.

Key Passage

So vanished and disappeared for ever a being who was protected by no one, who was dear to no one, who was of interest to no one.

Extract

In the department of — but I had better not mention which department. There is nothing in the world more touchy than a department, a regiment, a government office, and, in fact, any sort of official body. Nowadays every private individual considers all society insulted in his person. I have been told that very lately a complaint was received from a police inspector of which town I don't recollect, and that in this complaint he set forth clearly that the institutions of the State were in danger and that his sacred name was being taken in vain; and, in proof thereof, he appended to his complaint an enormously long volume of some romantic work in which a police inspector appeared on every tenth page, occasionally, indeed, in an intoxicated condition.

Public domain. First published in 1842. Translated from Russian.

About Nikolai Gogol

Nikolai Gogol was born on March 31, 1809, in Sorochyntsi, Ukraine, then part of the Russian Empire. His works — 'The Overcoat,' 'The Nose,' Dead Souls, and The Inspector General — established the satirical and absurdist tradition in Russian literature. Dostoevsky's famous remark, 'We all came out from under Gogol's Overcoat,' captures his foundational importance. He died in Moscow in 1852.
