The Celebrated Jumping Frog of Calaveras County

Mark Twain · Written 1865, Published 1865 · ~2,600 words

Published in 1865 in the New York Saturday Press, this was Mark Twain's first nationally celebrated story. A stranger from the East is told by a garrulous miner named Simon Wheeler about Jim Smiley, who would bet on anything — including his trained jumping frog Dan'l Webster, which a stranger fills with quail shot so it cannot jump.

Quick Summary

'The Celebrated Jumping Frog of Calaveras County' is a story by Mark Twain, published in 1865. The narrator is trapped by the verbose Wheeler into hearing about Jim Smiley, who bets obsessively on everything. Smiley has trained a jumping frog named Dan'l Webster. A stranger fills the frog with quail shot when Smiley is away fetching the stranger a frog to bet against — and Dan'l cannot jump. Smiley loses and never understands why.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

A frame story — the narrator tells the story Wheeler tells, and Wheeler's vernacular voice is the real subject of the comedy.

Themes

The comedy of obsessive gambling and American tall-tale tradition — the joke is as much on the narrator who has to sit through Wheeler as on Smiley.

Historical Context

Published November 18, 1865. The story made Twain nationally famous and launched his career.

Extract

In compliance with the request of a friend of mine, who wrote me from the East, I called on good-natured, garrulous old Simon Wheeler, and inquired after my friend's friend, Leonidas W. Smiley, as requested to do, and I hereunto append the result. I have a lurking suspicion that Leonidas W. Smiley is a myth; that my friend never knew such a personage; and that he only conjectured that if I asked old Wheeler about him, it would remind him of his infamous Jim Smiley, and he would go to work and bore me to death with some exasperating reminiscence of him as long and as tedious as it should be useless to me.

Public domain. First published in the New York Saturday Press, November 18, 1865.

About Mark Twain

Samuel Langhorne Clemens, known as Mark Twain, was born on November 30, 1835, in Florida, Missouri. The Adventures of Tom Sawyer (1876), Adventures of Huckleberry Finn (1884), and The Adventures of Tom Sawyer are among the most celebrated American novels. His satirical stories and essays make him the greatest American humorist. He died in Redding, Connecticut, in 1910.
