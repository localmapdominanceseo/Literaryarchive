Good Country People

Flannery O'Connor · Written 1955, Published 1955 · ~8,500 words

Published in 1955 in A Good Man Is Hard to Find, 'Good Country People' features Joy-Hulga Hopewell, a bitter thirty-two-year-old PhD in philosophy with a wooden leg and a heart condition, who lives with her cheerful, platitude-spouting mother. A Bible salesman named Manley Pointer seduces her and steals her wooden leg.

Quick Summary

'Good Country People' is a story by Flannery O'Connor, published in 1955. Joy-Hulga, a nihilistic PhD in philosophy, believes herself intellectually superior to everyone around her. A Bible salesman named Manley Pointer flirts with her; she plans to seduce and educate him. Instead, in the barn loft, he steals her wooden leg and reveals he collects odd things, including a woman's glass eye. He leaves her stranded. Her pride and her nihilism are literally taken from her.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

Third-person narration that moves between the perspectives of Mrs. Hopewell and Joy-Hulga, O'Connor's characteristic satirical gaze falling equally on both.

Themes

The pride of the intellectual who believes she has no illusions — Joy-Hulga is humiliated by her belief in her own superiority. Manley Pointer, the 'good country people' she despises, turns out to be more nihilistic than she is.

Historical Context

Published in A Good Man Is Hard to Find (1955). One of O'Connor's finest stories.

Extract

Besides the neutral expression that she wore when she was alone, Mrs. Freeman had two others, forward and reverse, that she used for all her human dealings. Her forward expression was steady and driving like the advance of a heavy truck. Her eyes never swerved to left or right but turned as the story turned as if they followed a yellow line down the center of it.

Published 1955. Copyright status: under copyright. Extract shown for educational commentary.

About Flannery O'Connor

Flannery O'Connor was born on March 25, 1925, in Savannah, Georgia. A devout Catholic writing in the Protestant South, she produced two novels and thirty-two short stories that combined grotesque comedy with fierce theological insight. Her most celebrated works include 'A Good Man Is Hard to Find' and 'The Violent Bear It Away.' She died of lupus in Milledgeville, Georgia, in 1964.
