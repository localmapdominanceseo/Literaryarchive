The Call of Cthulhu

H.P. Lovecraft · Written 1926, Published 1928 · ~12,000 words

Published in 1928 in Weird Tales, 'The Call of Cthulhu' is H.P. Lovecraft's most famous story. The narrator pieces together accounts from his dead uncle's papers, a sculptor's visions, a Louisiana cult, and a sailor's encounter at sea — all pointing to the sunken city of R'lyeh and its sleeping god Cthulhu, who sends dreams to sensitive minds.

Quick Summary

'The Call of Cthulhu' is a story by H.P. Lovecraft, published in 1928. The narrator discovers his dead uncle's research connecting strange dreams, a sculptor's clay tablets, a Louisiana voodoo cult, and a sailor's account of a briefly risen city in the Pacific — all pointing to the ancient cosmic entity Cthulhu, who sleeps in the sunken city of R'lyeh and whose awakening would drive humanity mad.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

Frame narrative assembled from documents — the mosaic structure enacts the story's theme: humanity assembling fragments of a truth too large to be seen whole.

Themes

Cosmic horror — the terror not of monsters but of the realization that humanity is utterly insignificant in a universe populated by indifferent entities of incomprehensible power. Lovecraft called this 'cosmicism.'

Historical Context

Written in 1926, published in Weird Tales, February 1928. The foundational text of the Cthulhu Mythos.

Opening Extract

The most merciful thing in the world, I think, is the inability of the human mind to correlate all its contents. We live on a placid island of ignorance in the midst of black seas of infinity, and it was not meant that we should voyage far. The sciences, each straining in its own direction, have hitherto harmed us little; but some day the piecing together of dissociated knowledge will open up such terrifying vistas of reality, and of our frightful position therein, that we shall either go mad from the revelation or flee from the deadly light into the peace and safety of a new dark age.

Public domain. First published in Weird Tales, February 1928.

About H.P. Lovecraft

Howard Phillips Lovecraft was born on August 20, 1890, in Providence, Rhode Island. He created the Cthulhu Mythos — a shared fictional universe of cosmic horror — through stories including 'The Call of Cthulhu,' 'At the Mountains of Madness,' and 'The Shadow over Innsmouth.' Largely unknown in his lifetime, he became enormously influential after his death in 1937.
