Flight

John Steinbeck · Written 1938, Published 1938 · ~9,500 words

Published in 1938 in The Long Valley, 'Flight' is a stark naturalist story about Pepé Torres, a nineteen-year-old Mexican-American boy who kills a man in a drunken quarrel and flees into the mountains. The mountains strip him of everything — his horse, his rifle, his food, his humanity — until he is finally shot.

Quick Summary

'Flight' is a story by John Steinbeck, published in 1938. Pepé Torres, nineteen, kills a man in Monterey after being called a name. He flees into the mountains. The mountains take everything from him — his hat, his horse, his rifle, his water, his hand which must be cut off. He climbs until he can climb no more and stands upright to meet the bullet that kills him.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

Third-person naturalist narration — the mountains are as much a character as Pepé, stripping him down methodically.

Themes

A young man's fatal initiation into manhood — Pepé's journey is a rite of passage that kills him. Steinbeck shows nature as indifferent and relentless.

Historical Context

Published in The Long Valley (1938). Frequently anthologized.

Extract

The road came out of the oaks and wound over the brown hills toward the little pueblo of Tortilla Flat. Pepé Torres sat on the tailboard of a dilapidated wagon. He was a fine, lean boy, nineteen years old, long-limbed and black-haired. He held a five-cent piece in his hand and he was turning it between his fingers.

About John Steinbeck

John Steinbeck was born on February 27, 1902, in Salinas, California. His novels of working-class California life — Of Mice and Men (1937), The Grapes of Wrath (1939), and East of Eden (1952) — established him as one of the great American writers. He won the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1962. He died in New York in 1968.
