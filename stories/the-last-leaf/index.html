The Last Leaf

O. Henry · Written 1907, Published 1907 · ~2,500 words

Published in 1907 in The Trimmed Lamp, 'The Last Leaf' is O. Henry's most celebrated story of sacrifice. A young woman sick with pneumonia has decided she will die when the last leaf falls from the ivy vine outside her window. An old artist paints a leaf on the wall in the cold and rain. She lives. He dies of pneumonia.

Quick Summary

'The Last Leaf' is a story by O. Henry, published in 1907. Johnsy, sick with pneumonia, has decided she will die when the last leaf falls from the ivy outside her window. Old Behrman, a failed artist who has talked for years of painting a masterpiece, goes out in the storm and paints a leaf on the brick wall. Johnsy recovers, thinking the leaf survived. Behrman dies of pneumonia — the leaf was his masterpiece.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

Third-person narration with O. Henry's characteristic withheld information and twist ending — the revelation of Behrman's sacrifice comes through a third party.

Themes

Selfless sacrifice and the unexpected masterpiece — Behrman's life's work turns out to be not a painting on canvas but an act of love.

Historical Context

Published in The Trimmed Lamp (1907). One of O. Henry's most anthologized stories.

Opening Extract

In a little district west of Washington Square the streets have run crazy and broken themselves into small strips called 'places.' These 'places' make strange angles and curves. One Street crosses itself a time or two. An artist once discovered a valuable possibility in this street. Suppose a collector with a bill for paints, paper and canvas should, in traversing this route, suddenly meet himself coming back, without a cent having been paid on account!

Public domain. Published in The Trimmed Lamp, 1907.

About O. Henry

William Sydney Porter, known by his pen name O. Henry, was born on September 11, 1862, in Greensboro, North Carolina. He wrote nearly 600 short stories celebrated for their wit, wordplay, and surprise twist endings. His most famous works include 'The Gift of the Magi' and 'The Last Leaf.' He died in New York in 1910.
