The Garden of Forking Paths

Jorge Luis Borges · Written 1941, Published 1941 · ~4,500 words

Published in 1941 in the collection El jardín de senderos que se bifurcan, 'The Garden of Forking Paths' is a spy story and a philosophical puzzle. Dr. Yu Tsun, a Chinese spy working for Germany in World War I England, must transmit the name of an artillery park to his handlers — and does so by murdering a man whose name is the same as the town.

Quick Summary

'The Garden of Forking Paths' is a story by Jorge Luis Borges, published in 1941. Dr. Yu Tsun, a German spy, must pass information without being caught. He visits Stephen Albert, a sinologist who has solved the mystery of Yu Tsun's ancestor's labyrinth — it is a novel and a garden that represents all possible futures simultaneously, all existing in parallel. Yu Tsun kills Albert, whose name is reported in the newspaper — that is the message to Berlin about the artillery park at Albert.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

A spy thriller that contains a philosophical essay within it — the embedded explanation of the forking paths labyrinth is the story's true subject.

Themes

The coexistence of all possible time lines — Ts'ui Pen's garden of forking paths is a novel in which every possible outcome occurs in some branch. The story asks whether individual choices matter if every choice is simultaneously made.

Historical Context

Published in El jardín de senderos que se bifurcan (1941). Collected in Ficciones (1944). One of the foundational texts of postmodern fiction.

Extract

I thought of a labyrinth of labyrinths, of one sinuous spreading labyrinth that would encompass the past and the future and in some way involve the stars. Absorbed in these illusory images, I forgot my destiny of one pursued. I felt myself to be, for an unknown period of time, an abstract perceiver of the world. The vague, living countryside, the moon, the remains of the day worked on me, as well as the slope of the road which eliminated any possibility of weariness.

Public domain in many jurisdictions. Published 1941. Translated from Spanish.

About Jorge Luis Borges

Jorge Luis Borges was born on August 24, 1899, in Buenos Aires, Argentina. His short story collections Ficciones (1944) and El Aleph (1949) established him as one of the most influential fiction writers of the twentieth century. His labyrinthine, philosophical stories — mixing detective fiction, fantasy, and metaphysics — founded what would become known as magical realism. He died in Geneva in 1986.
