Exhalation

Ted Chiang · Written 2008, Published 2008 · ~7,500 words

Published in 2008 in Eclipse Two, Ted Chiang's 'Exhalation' is narrated by a being of pressurized argon who dissects their own brain to discover why the world is running down. The answer: the universe tends toward equilibrium, and when all pressures equalize, all thought and life will stop.

Quick Summary

'Exhalation' is a story by Ted Chiang, published in 2008. A being of pressurized argon narrates how they dissected their own brain and discovered that thought is powered by the pressure differential between their world's air and the reservoir beneath it. As that pressure equalizes over time, all thought will slow and stop. The story meditates on entropy, consciousness, and leaving something beautiful for whatever comes after.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

A scientific memoir addressed to future readers — the form of a scientific paper filtered through profound emotion.

Themes

Entropy and the love of existence — the narrator accepts the inevitable heat death of their universe but chooses to leave a record of its beauty. The story is about finding meaning in a finite system.

Historical Context

Published in Eclipse Two (2008). Won the Hugo and British Science Fiction Association Awards.

Opening Extract

I have been thinking about the end of the world.

This is not a morbid obsession but simply a matter of arithmetic. The universe, the world I inhabit, has a finite supply of air; when that air has been used, all motion will cease and the world will stop. I know this because I have examined the ground upon which we walk and the air through which we fly and the walls that bound our world.

Published 2008. Copyright status: under copyright. Extract shown for educational commentary.

About Ted Chiang

Ted Chiang was born on October 20, 1967, in Port Jefferson, New York. He is one of the most celebrated contemporary short story writers in any genre, known for the extraordinary precision of his ideas and prose. His collections Stories of Your Life and Others (2002) and Exhalation (2019) have won multiple Hugo and Nebula Awards. 'Story of Your Life' was adapted as the film Arrival (2016).
