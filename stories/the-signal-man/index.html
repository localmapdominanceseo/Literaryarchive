The Signal-Man

Charles Dickens · Written 1866, Published 1866 · ~6,500 words

Published in 1866 in All the Year Round, 'The Signal-Man' is Charles Dickens's most celebrated ghost story. A railway signal-man working alone in a deep cutting has been visited three times by a spectre that appears before the cutting — each visit preceding a terrible accident. The narrator visits him repeatedly; then learns the signal-man has been killed by a train.

Quick Summary

'The Signal-Man' is a ghost story by Dickens, published in 1866. A railway signal-man working in a gloomy cutting has been visited by a spectre that appears before accidents on the line. He cannot understand why the ghost does not warn him more clearly. On the narrator's final visit, he learns the signal-man was killed by a train — and the driver's warning cry as he approached was exactly the words and gesture the ghost had used.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

First-person narration — the narrator's initial skepticism gives way to unease, and the story's ending provides no comfort.

Themes

Fatalism and the inability of warnings to prevent disaster — the ghost warns but cannot save. The story is also about the isolation of the railway worker, buried in his cutting.

Historical Context

Published in the Christmas 1866 edition of All the Year Round. Often cited as one of the finest Victorian ghost stories.

Opening Extract

'Halloa! Below there!'

When he heard a voice thus calling to him, he was standing at the door of his box, with a flag in his hand, furled round its short pole. One would have thought, considering the nature of the ground, that he could not have doubted from what quarter the voice came; but instead of looking up to where I stood on the top of the steep cutting nearly over his head, he turned himself about, and looked down the Line.

Public domain. Published in All the Year Round, Christmas 1866.

About Charles Dickens

Charles Dickens was born on February 7, 1812, in Portsmouth, England. The greatest Victorian novelist, he wrote Oliver Twist, A Tale of Two Cities, Great Expectations, and many other novels serialized for mass audiences. He is also one of the finest writers of ghost stories, including 'A Christmas Carol' and 'The Signal-Man.' He died in Gad's Hill Place in 1870.
