The Chrysanthemums

John Steinbeck · Written 1937, Published 1937 · ~4,200 words

Published in 1937 in Harper's Magazine, 'The Chrysanthemums' is widely considered Steinbeck's finest story. Elisa Allen, a strong, energetic woman trapped in a stifling ranch marriage, responds intensely to a passing tinker who shows interest in her chrysanthemums — the one outlet for her passion. Later she sees her plants discarded on the road.

Quick Summary

'The Chrysanthemums' is a story by John Steinbeck, published in 1937. Elisa Allen tends her chrysanthemum garden with fierce energy while her husband tends the ranch business. A wandering tinker flatters her interest and she gives him a pot of chrysanthemum shoots. Later, driving to dinner with her husband, she sees the shoots thrown out on the road — he took the pot but not the plants. She cries quietly.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

Close third-person narration — Steinbeck's prose captures physical detail with extraordinary precision, the natural world mirroring Elisa's interior life.

Themes

Frustrated desire and the constrained lives of women — Elisa's passionate energy has no outlet beyond her garden. The tinker momentarily offers connection before showing he was only interested in the pot.

Historical Context

Published in Harper's Magazine, October 1937. Collected in The Long Valley (1938).

Extract

The high grey-flannel fog of winter closed off the Salinas Valley from the sky and from all the rest of the world. On every side it sat like a lid on the mountains and made of the great valley a closed pot. On the broad, level land floor the gang ploughs bit deep and left the black earth shining like metal where the shares had cut.

Published 1937. Copyright status: under copyright. Extract shown for educational commentary.

About John Steinbeck

John Steinbeck was born on February 27, 1902, in Salinas, California. His novels of working-class California life — Of Mice and Men (1937), The Grapes of Wrath (1939), and East of Eden (1952) — established him as one of the great American writers. He won the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1962. He died in New York in 1968.
