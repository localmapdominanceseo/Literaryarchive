We Have Always Lived in the Castle (Extract)

Shirley Jackson · Written 1962, Published 1962 · ~53,000 words

Published in 1962, 'We Have Always Lived in the Castle' is Shirley Jackson's final novel. Narrated by eighteen-year-old Merricat Blackwood, who lives with her sister Constance and uncle Julian in their isolated family estate, the novel gradually reveals that Merricat poisoned the rest of her family six years earlier — and that her sister protected her.

Quick Summary

'We Have Always Lived in the Castle' is a novel by Shirley Jackson, published in 1962. Merricat Blackwood lives with her sister Constance and uncle Julian in their isolated house. The village hates them because someone poisoned the family six years ago. Merricat's narration gradually reveals she was the poisoner — and her sister Constance, who was tried and acquitted, has protected her ever since.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

First-person narration by Merricat — whose magical thinking, rituals, and hatred of the outside world are presented as charming eccentricities until their true nature becomes clear.

Themes

The castle as refuge from a hostile world, sisterly devotion, and the psychology of the outsider — Jackson explores how the Blackwood sisters have created a self-sufficient world that the outside keeps trying to destroy.

Historical Context

Published 1962. Jackson's last novel, written while she was ill. Widely considered her finest work.

Opening Extract

My name is Mary Katherine Blackwood. I am eighteen years old, and I live with my sister Constance. I have often thought that with any luck at all I could have been born a werewolf, because the two middle fingers on both my hands are the same length, but I have had to be content with what I am. I dislike washing myself, and dogs, and noise. I like my sister Constance, and Richard Plantagenet, and Amanita phalloides, the death-cup mushroom. Everyone else in my family is dead.

Published 1962. Copyright status: under copyright. Extract shown for educational commentary.

About Shirley Jackson

Shirley Jackson was born on December 14, 1916, in San Francisco. Her story 'The Lottery' (1948) provoked more reader mail than any piece The New Yorker had published. Her novel The Haunting of Hill House (1959) is considered one of the finest haunted house stories ever written. She died in North Bennington, Vermont, in 1965.
