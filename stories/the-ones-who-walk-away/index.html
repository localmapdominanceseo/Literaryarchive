Story of Your Life

Ted Chiang · Written 1998, Published 1998 · ~14,000 words

Published in 1998 in Starlight 2, Ted Chiang's 'Story of Your Life' follows Dr. Louise Banks, a linguist who learns the alien language of the Heptapods and finds that learning it restructures her perception of time — she begins to experience her life simultaneously, including the future death of her daughter.

Quick Summary

'Story of Your Life' is a story by Ted Chiang, published in 1998. Linguist Louise Banks learns the alien Heptapod language, which is structured around simultaneous rather than sequential time. As she learns it, her perception of time changes — she begins to perceive future and past simultaneously. The story is addressed to her daughter, who will die young in a hiking accident. Louise knows this and chooses to have her anyway.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

Alternating between the narrative present of alien first contact and addresses to the future daughter — the formal structure enacts the story's theme of non-linear time.

Themes

Free will and determinism — if you know the future, can you choose differently? Louise chooses to have her daughter knowing she will die. The story argues that love is not diminished by foreknowledge.

Historical Context

Published in Starlight 2 (1998). Won the Nebula Award for Best Novella. Adapted as the film Arrival (2016).

Extract

Your father is about to ask me the question. This is the most important moment in our lives, and I want to pay attention, note every detail. Your dad and I have just come back from an evening out, dinner and a show; it's after midnight. We came out onto the porch afterward to look at the full moon. He'll go in to use the bathroom soon, and I'll be alone out here.

About Ted Chiang

Ted Chiang was born on October 20, 1967, in Port Jefferson, New York. He is one of the most celebrated contemporary short story writers in any genre, known for the extraordinary precision of his ideas and prose. His collections Stories of Your Life and Others (2002) and Exhalation (2019) have won multiple Hugo and Nebula Awards. 'Story of Your Life' was adapted as the film Arrival (2016).
