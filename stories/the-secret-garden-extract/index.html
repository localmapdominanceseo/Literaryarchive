The Necklace of Princess Fiorimonde

Mary De Morgan · Written 1880, Published 1880 · ~4,500 words

Published in 1880, 'The Necklace of Princess Fiorimonde' is Mary De Morgan's most celebrated fairy tale. The wicked Princess Fiorimonde turns her suitors into beads on her necklace. A young page named Ghoulem discovers the secret and must free them.

Quick Summary

'The Necklace of Princess Fiorimonde' is a fairy tale by Mary De Morgan, published in 1880. The beautiful but wicked Princess Fiorimonde turns every suitor who comes to court her into a bead on her necklace using dark magic. A young page named Ghoulem discovers the truth and, with the help of a wise woman, breaks the spell and restores the princes.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

Traditional fairy tale structure with a female villain and male rescuer reversed in interesting ways.

Themes

The corruption of beauty and vanity — Fiorimonde's cruelty is her defining characteristic, and the story is a moral fable about the consequences of treating people as objects.

Historical Context

Published in The Necklace of Princess Fiorimonde and Other Stories (1880). De Morgan was a significant Victorian fairy tale writer, friend of William Morris.

Opening Extract

Once upon a time, in a country far away, there lived a King who had one daughter. She was so beautiful that all who saw her loved her, yet she had a cruel heart, and cared for no one but herself. Her name was Fiorimonde, and from the time she was quite a little child, she had thought of nothing but how she might get power over others, and how she might always have her own way.

About Mary De Morgan

Mary De Morgan was born in 1850 in London, sister of the artist William De Morgan and friend of William Morris. She wrote three collections of fairy tales celebrated for their moral complexity and literary quality. She died in Cairo in 1907.
