The Adventure of the Red-Headed League

Arthur Conan Doyle · Written 1891, Published 1891 · ~9,000 words

Published in 1891 in The Strand Magazine, 'The Adventure of the Red-Headed League' has Jabez Wilson consulting Holmes about a bizarre society that paid him to copy out the encyclopedia — a scheme Holmes immediately recognises as a distraction while criminals tunnel into a bank vault.

Quick Summary

'The Adventure of the Red-Headed League' is a Sherlock Holmes story by Conan Doyle, published in 1891. Jabez Wilson was recruited into the Red-Headed League, which paid him well to copy encyclopedias. The League suddenly dissolves. Holmes deduces it was a scheme to keep Wilson away from his pawnshop while his assistant and an accomplice tunnel into the bank vault next door.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

Watson's first-person narration, following the standard Holmes structure of problem, investigation, and revelation.

Themes

Misdirection and the criminal mastermind — the elaborate scheme to keep Wilson occupied is an example of Conan Doyle's delight in ingenious plots.

Historical Context

Published in The Strand Magazine, August 1891. One of the most famous Holmes stories.

Opening Extract

I had called upon my friend Mr. Sherlock Holmes one day in the autumn of last year and found him in deep conversation with a very stout, florid-faced, elderly gentleman with fiery red hair. With an apology for my intrusion, I was about to withdraw when Holmes pulled me abruptly into the room and closed the door behind me.

'You could not possibly have come at a better time, my dear Watson,' he said cordially.

Public domain. First published in The Strand Magazine, August 1891.

About Arthur Conan Doyle

Arthur Conan Doyle was born on May 22, 1859, in Edinburgh, Scotland. He created Sherlock Holmes, the most famous fictional detective in the world, in A Study in Scarlet (1887). He wrote four novels and 56 short stories featuring Holmes. He died in Crowborough, England, in 1930.
