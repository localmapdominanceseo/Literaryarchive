The Beast in the Jungle

Henry James · Written 1903, Published 1903 · ~18,000 words

Published in 1903 in The Better Sort, 'The Beast in the Jungle' is Henry James's most celebrated novella. John Marcher is convinced that something rare and extraordinary — a beast crouching in the jungle — is going to happen to him. He confides this to May Bartram, who agrees to wait and watch with him. She dies. Years later, at her grave, he understands: the beast was that he had loved her and felt nothing.

Quick Summary

'The Beast in the Jungle' is a novella by Henry James, published in 1903. John Marcher is convinced a great event is destined to happen to him. May Bartram agrees to share his vigil. Years pass. She grows ill and dies — having known what the beast was. At her graveside, watching a grieving stranger, Marcher understands: the beast was that nothing happened to him because he could love nothing. He had failed May Bartram utterly.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

Close third-person narration in James's late style — dense, circling, the meaning always slightly ahead of what is directly stated.

Themes

The failure of consciousness to recognize love and life while they are present — Marcher's great destiny was precisely that he had none, because he could never give himself to another person. The story is about egotism and emotional paralysis.

Historical Context

Published in The Better Sort (1903). James's most personal and most devastating story about the failure to live.

Key Passage

The escape would have been to love her; then, then he would have lived.

Opening Extract

What determined the speech that startled him in the course of their encounter scarcely matters, being probably but some words she spoke of herself, something that fell from her as to their old friendship that would have served any way as the opening he needed. She had been led to it probably by being asked her if she remembered their particular old talk together — she could scarcely not have forgotten it — in the garden-party of Mrs. Waddell's, in the country, where they had met for the first time, at the age of two-and-twenty. It was this reminiscence that served as the bridge.

Public domain. First published in The Better Sort, 1903.

About Henry James

Henry James was born on April 15, 1843, in New York City. He became an American citizen of England (he moved to England in 1876 and was naturalized in 1915). His fiction — including The Portrait of a Lady (1881), The Turn of the Screw (1898), and 'The Beast in the Jungle' (1903) — explores consciousness, perception, and the moral life with unparalleled subtlety. He died in Chelsea, London, in 1916.
