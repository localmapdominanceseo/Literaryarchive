The Adventure of the Speckled Band

Arthur Conan Doyle · Written 1892, Published 1892 · ~9,500 words

Published in 1892 in The Strand Magazine, 'The Adventure of the Speckled Band' is widely considered the best Sherlock Holmes story. Helen Stoner fears she will die in the same mysterious circumstances as her twin sister Julia, who died two years earlier whispering 'the speckled band.' Holmes investigates the locked-room mystery.

Quick Summary

'The Adventure of the Speckled Band' is a Sherlock Holmes story by Arthur Conan Doyle, published in 1892. Helen Stoner fears her stepfather Dr. Roylott is planning to kill her as he killed her sister Julia. Holmes deduces that Julia was killed by a swamp adder trained to travel through a ventilator and down a bell rope — and turns the trap on Roylott himself.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

First-person narration by Watson — the classic Holmes story structure: client presents problem, Holmes investigates, Holmes explains solution.

Themes

The brilliant detective as protector of the vulnerable, and the gothic villain defeated by pure reason.

Historical Context

Published in The Strand Magazine, February 1892. Conan Doyle himself ranked it as his favourite Holmes story.

Opening Extract

'Good morning,' said Holmes cheerily. 'I am glad to see that Mrs. Hudson has had the good sense to light the fire. Pray draw up to it, and I shall order you a cup of hot coffee, for I observe that you are shivering.'

'It is not cold which makes me shiver,' said the woman in a low voice, changing her seat as requested.

'What, then?'

'It is fear, Mr. Holmes. It is terror.'

Public domain. First published in The Strand Magazine, February 1892.

About Arthur Conan Doyle

Arthur Conan Doyle was born on May 22, 1859, in Edinburgh, Scotland. He created Sherlock Holmes, the most famous fictional detective in the world, in A Study in Scarlet (1887). He wrote four novels and 56 short stories featuring Holmes. He died in Crowborough, England, in 1930.
