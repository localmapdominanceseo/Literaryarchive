The Turn of the Screw

Henry James · Written 1898, Published 1898 · ~42,000 words

Published in 1898 in Collier's Weekly, 'The Turn of the Screw' is Henry James's most celebrated novella of horror. A young governess takes charge of two children at a remote English estate. She begins seeing ghosts of former servants. The children seem to know the ghosts and to be hiding something. The governess becomes increasingly desperate to protect the children — with fatal results.

Quick Summary

'The Turn of the Screw' is a novella by Henry James, published in 1898. A governess at Bly believes she sees the ghosts of two dead former servants — Quint and Miss Jessel — and that they are corrupting the two children in her care. The children deny seeing anything. The governess becomes increasingly frantic. At the climax she confronts Miles, demanding he admit seeing Quint. Miles dies in her arms.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

A frame narrative within a frame narrative — giving the story maximum distance and ambiguity. The governess's account is presented as a manuscript, never verified.

Themes

The story is deliberately ambiguous about whether the ghosts are real or products of the governess's disturbed imagination. James refused to clarify, making the ambiguity central to the horror.

Historical Context

Published in Collier's Weekly in 1898. One of the most debated stories in English literature for its central ambiguity.

Opening Extract

The story had held us, round the fire, sufficiently breathless, but except the obvious remark that it was gruesome, as, on Christmas eve in an old house, a strange tale should essentially be, I remember no comment uttered till somebody happened to say that it was the only case he had met in which such a visitation had fallen on a child. The case, I may mention, was that of an apparition in just such an old house as had gathered us for the occasion — an appearance, of a dreadful kind, to a little boy sleeping in the room with his mother and waking her up in the terror of it.

Public domain. First published in Collier's Weekly, 1898.

About Henry James

Henry James was born on April 15, 1843, in New York City. He became an American citizen of England (he moved to England in 1876 and was naturalized in 1915). His fiction — including The Portrait of a Lady (1881), The Turn of the Screw (1898), and 'The Beast in the Jungle' (1903) — explores consciousness, perception, and the moral life with unparalleled subtlety. He died in Chelsea, London, in 1916.
