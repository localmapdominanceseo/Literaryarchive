The Furnished Room

O. Henry · Written 1904, Published 1904 · ~2,900 words

Published in 1904 in The Four Million, 'The Furnished Room' is O. Henry's darkest story. A young man searches New York furnished rooms for a girl singer he loves. He rents a room where he senses her presence — and the landlady says no girl of that description has stayed there. He turns on the gas. The landladies downstairs discuss the girl who killed herself there a week ago.

Quick Summary

'The Furnished Room' is a story by O. Henry, published in 1904. A young man searches furnished rooms in New York for a girl singer. He rents a room where he senses something of her — a fragment of her perfume, the feeling of her presence. The landlady says she doesn't recall such a girl. He turns on the gas and kills himself. The two landladies downstairs discuss the girl who gassed herself in that same room a week before.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

Third-person narration with O. Henry's characteristic twist ending — here deployed for tragedy rather than comedy.

Themes

The loneliness of the city and the tragic near-miss — the two lovers were in the same room a week apart and never knew.

Historical Context

Published in The Four Million (1906). One of O. Henry's most affecting stories.

Opening Extract

The young man thrust himself in between the bulging mattresses and the writing desk. He opened the little door of the stove and thrust his head inside. It was dark, but he could feel the faint, stale odour of gas that lingered.

Public domain. Published in The Four Million, 1906.

About O. Henry

William Sydney Porter, known by his pen name O. Henry, was born on September 11, 1862, in Greensboro, North Carolina. He wrote nearly 600 short stories celebrated for their wit, wordplay, and surprise twist endings. His most famous works include 'The Gift of the Magi' and 'The Last Leaf.' He died in New York in 1910.
