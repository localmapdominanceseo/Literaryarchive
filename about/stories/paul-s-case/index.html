Paul's Case

Willa Cather · Written 1905, Published 1905 · ~8,500 words

Published in 1905 in The Troll Garden, 'Paul's Case' describes Paul, a Pittsburgh high school student who is a studied, theatrical liar with an obsessive love of beauty and music. Expelled from school, he steals money from his employer and spends a week living the luxurious New York life he has always dreamed of — before throwing himself under a train.

Quick Summary

'Paul's Case' is a story by Willa Cather, published in 1905. Paul, a strange, theatrically sensitive teenager in Pittsburgh, lives for Carnegie Hall and the theatre, despising his drab suburban life. Expelled from school and his job threatened, he steals money from his employer and goes to New York, where he lives a week of imagined luxury. When he reads his theft has been discovered, he throws himself under a train.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

Third-person narration with close sympathy for Paul's perspective — readers understand his hunger even as the story shows how destructive and dishonest it makes him.

Themes

The conflict between the desire for beauty and the conditions of ordinary life — Paul cannot live in the world as it is, and cannot live in the world as he imagines it either.

Historical Context

Published in The Troll Garden (1905). Cather's most anthologized story.

Opening Extract

It was Paul's afternoon to appear before the faculty of the Pittsburgh High School to account for his various misdemeanours. He had been suspended a week ago, and his father had called at the Principal's office and confessed his perplexity about his son. Paul entered the faculty room suave and smiling. His clothes were a trifle outgrown, and the tan velvet on the collar of his open overcoat was frayed and worn; but for all that there was something of the dandy about him, and he wore an opal pin in his neatly knotted black four-in-hand, and a red carnation in his buttonhole.

Public domain. Published in The Troll Garden, 1905.

About Willa Cather

Willa Cather was born on December 7, 1873, in Back Creek Valley, Virginia. Her novels of the American frontier — O Pioneers! (1913), My Ántonia (1918), and Death Comes for the Archbishop (1927) — are among the most celebrated in American literature. 'Paul's Case' (1905) is her most anthologized story. She died in New York in 1947.
