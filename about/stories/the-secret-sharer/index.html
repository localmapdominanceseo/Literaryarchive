The Secret Sharer

Joseph Conrad · Written 1909, Published 1910 · ~18,000 words

Published in 1910, 'The Secret Sharer' is Joseph Conrad's novella about a young sea captain who finds a naked man clinging to the side of his ship at night. The man, Leggatt, has killed a sailor and escaped from the ship that was holding him. The captain hides Leggatt in his cabin for weeks, eventually helping him escape — seeing in the fugitive a double of himself.

Quick Summary

'The Secret Sharer' is a novella by Joseph Conrad, published in 1910. A young captain, new to his command, finds Leggatt — an escaped fugitive who killed a sailor — hanging off his ship's ladder at night. He hides Leggatt in his cabin as his double, his secret self. When the ship is close to the island where Leggatt can escape, the captain takes the risk of sailing dangerously close to shore. Leggatt swims away. The captain, having proved himself, steers the ship to safety.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

First-person retrospective narration — the captain tells the story from later, though the full meaning is only visible in retrospect.

Themes

The double and the split self — Leggatt is the captain's hidden self, the man capable of violence and decisive action that the captain has not yet tested. By sheltering him, the captain acknowledges and integrates this shadow self.

Historical Context

Published in Harper's Magazine in 1910. One of Conrad's most admired shorter works.

Key Passage

And I was alone with her. Nothing! No one in the world should stand now between us, throwing a shadow on the way of silent knowledge and mute affection, the perfect communion of a seaman with his first command.

Extract

On my right hand there were lines of fishing-stakes resembling a mysterious system of half-submerged bamboo fences, incomprehensible in its division of the domain of tropical fishes, and crazy of aspect as if abandoned forever by some nomad tribe of fishermen now gone to the other end of the ocean; for there was no sign of human habitation as far as the eye could reach. To the left a group of barren islets, suggesting ruins of stone walls, towers, and blockhouses, had its foundations set in a blue sea that itself looked solid, so still and stable did it lie below my feet; even the track of light from the westering sun shone smoothly, without that animated glitter which tells of an imperceptible ripple.

Public domain. First published in Harper's Magazine, 1910.

About Joseph Conrad

Joseph Conrad was born Józef Teodor Konrad Korzeniowski on December 3, 1857, in Berdychiv, Ukraine, then part of the Russian Empire. Polish by birth, he became one of the greatest English-language novelists, writing in his third language. Heart of Darkness (1899), Lord Jim (1900), and The Secret Sharer (1910) are among his masterpieces. He died in Bishopsbourne, England, in 1924.
