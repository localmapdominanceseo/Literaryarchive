The Monkey's Paw

W.W. Jacobs · Written 1902, Published 1902 · ~4,300 words

Published in 1902 in Harper's Monthly, 'The Monkey's Paw' is W.W. Jacobs's most famous horror story. A family receives a mummified monkey's paw that grants three wishes, but always with terrible consequences. They wish for two hundred pounds; their son is killed in a machinery accident and the compensation is exactly two hundred pounds. They wish him back from the dead. Then they wish again.

Quick Summary

'The Monkey's Paw' is a story by W.W. Jacobs, published in 1902. The White family receives a magical monkey's paw that grants three wishes but always with terrible consequences. Mr. White wishes for two hundred pounds — their son Herbert is killed in a factory accident and the compensation is exactly two hundred pounds. Mrs. White wishes Herbert back. Something comes to the door in the night. Mr. White makes the third wish — and the knocking stops. They open the door to find the road empty.

Literary Analysis

Form & Structure

Third-person narration, cozy and domestic in its setting, making the horror all the more effective when it arrives.

Themes

Fate and the price of interfering with it — the paw grants wishes but always extracts a terrible cost. The story is a parable about the danger of desiring what we do not have.

Historical Context

Published in Harper's Monthly in September 1902. One of the most frequently adapted horror stories ever written.

Key Passage

'For God's sake don't let it in,' cried the old man, trembling. 'I wish him dead.'

Opening Extract

Without, the night was cold and wet, but in the small parlour of Laburnum Villa the blinds were drawn and the fire burned brightly. Father and son were at chess, the former, who possessed ideas about the game involving radical changes, putting his king into such sharp and unnecessary perils that it even provoked comment from the white-haired old lady knitting placidly by the fire.

'Hark at the wind,' said Mr. White, who, having seen a fatal mistake after it was too late, was amiably desirous of preventing his son from seeing it.

'I'm listening,' said the latter, grimly surveying the board as he stretched out his hand. 'Check.'

'I should hardly think that he'd come to-night,' said his father, with his hand poised over the board.

'Mate,' replied the son.

'That's the worst of living so far out,' balled Mr. White, with sudden and unlooked-for violence; 'of all the beastly, slushy, out-of-the-way places to live in, this is the worst. Pathway's a bog, and the road's a torrent. I don't know what people are thinking about. I suppose because only two houses in the road are let, they think it doesn't matter.'

Public domain. First published in Harper's Monthly, September 1902.

About W.W. Jacobs

William Wymark Jacobs was born on September 8, 1863, in London. He is remembered primarily for one story — 'The Monkey's Paw' (1902) — which became one of the most frequently adapted horror stories ever written. He died in London in 1943.
