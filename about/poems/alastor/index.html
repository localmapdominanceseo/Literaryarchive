Alastor, or The Spirit of Solitude (Extract)

by Percy Bysshe Shelley

Written 1816 Published 1816 English Romanticism Public Domain

Published in 1816, Alastor is Shelley's first major poem — a narrative about a young poet who pursues a vision of ideal beauty across the world until it destroys him. The extract covers the invocation and the poet's death at the end.

Quick Answer

Alastor is Shelley's 1816 poem about a poet who pursues an ideal vision of beauty across Eastern landscapes until it kills him. Unable to find in the real world the beauty his imagination has conceived, he fades and dies by a beautiful stream. The poem debates whether such solitary idealism is noble or destructive.

Mother of this unfathomable world!
Favour my solemn song, for I have loved
Thee ever, and thee only; I have watched
Thy shadow, and the darkness of thy steps,
And my heart ever gazes on the depth
Of thy deep mysteries. I have made my bed
In charnels and on coffins, where black death
Keeps record of the trophies won from thee,
Hoping to still these obstinate questionings
Of thee and thine, by forcing some lone ghost,
Thy messenger, to render up the tale
Of what we are. In lone and silent hours,
When night makes a weird sound of its own stillness,
Like an inspired and desperate alchemist
Staking his very life on some dark hope,
Have I mixed awful talk and asking looks
With my most innocent love, until strange tears
Uniting with those breathless kisses, made
Such magic as compels the charmed night
To render up thy charge: and, though ne'er yet
Thou hast unveiled thy inmost sanctuary,
Enough from incommunicable dream,
And twilight phantasms, and deep noon-day thought,
Has shone within me, that serenely now
And moveless, as a long-forgotten lyre
Suspended in the solitary dome
Of some mysterious and deserted fane,
I wait thy breath, Great Parent.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Seven hundred and twenty lines of blank verse, modeled on the Miltonic tradition of blank verse narrative.

Themes

The dangers of ideal beauty — the poet's pursuit of a perfect beauty he can never find destroys him. Shelley is both celebrating and critiquing the Romantic ideal of the solitary, visionary artist.

Historical Context

Published in 1816. Shelley wrote it while living near Windsor Great Park. The title comes from the Greek word for an avenging demon.

About Percy Bysshe Shelley

Percy Bysshe Shelley was born on August 4, 1792, in Horsham, England, and became one of the major poets of English Romanticism despite controversy during his lifetime over his radical political and religious views. His poetry combines lyrical intensity with sustained engagement with politics, philosophy, and the natural world. He drowned in a boating accident off the coast of Italy in 1822, at the age of twenty-nine.
