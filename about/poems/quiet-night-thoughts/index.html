Quiet Night Thoughts

by Li Bai

Written 726 Published 726 Tang Dynasty Poetry Public Domain

Written around 726 CE, this is one of the most memorized poems in the Chinese language, describing a traveler who sees moonlight on the floor and mistakes it for frost, then lifts his head to look at the moon and bows it thinking of home.

Quick Answer

"Quiet Night Thoughts" is a poem by Li Bai, written around 726 CE. Moonlight falls before the speaker's bed like frost on the ground. He raises his head to gaze at the bright moon, then lowers it and thinks of home. In twenty Chinese characters, it captures the essential longing of the traveler far from home.

Before my bed the bright moonlight gleams,
I thought it was frost on the ground.
I raise my head to gaze at the bright moon,
I lower my head and think of my old home.

Public domain translation. Original c.726 CE by Li Bai (701-762 CE). Translation is a free rendering in the public domain.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Five lines of five characters each in the ancient-style verse (gushi) tradition, simple and spare.

Themes

Homesickness and the moon as a symbol connecting the traveler to home and loved ones. The moon is the same moon visible everywhere — looking at it in one place is a way of connecting to those who see it in another.

Historical Context

Li Bai (701-762 CE) is considered one of the greatest Chinese poets. This poem is among the most memorized in the Chinese-speaking world, learned by schoolchildren for over a thousand years. This prose rendering is in the public domain.

About Li Bai

Li Bai (701-762 CE), also known as Li Po, is one of the two greatest poets of the Tang Dynasty. He was famous in his own lifetime for his Taoist-influenced celebration of wine, the moon, nature, and friendship. His poetry is known for its imaginative freedom, its musical qualities, and its range from playful to sublime.
