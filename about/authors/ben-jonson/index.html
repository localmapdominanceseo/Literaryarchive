Ben Jonson

1572–1637 British English Renaissance

Ben Jonson was born on June 11, 1572, in London, and became the foremost dramatist and poet of the Jacobean era, second only to Shakespeare in contemporary reputation and arguably his superior in formal learning and critical theory. His plays Volpone (1606) and The Alchemist (1610) are among the greatest comedies in English. As a poet, his Epigrams, The Forest, and Under-wood collections display an unrivaled technical mastery and classical learning. He was effectively the first Poet Laureate of England. He died in London in 1637.
