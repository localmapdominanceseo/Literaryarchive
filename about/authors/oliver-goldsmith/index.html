Oliver Goldsmith

1728–1774 Irish English Romanticism

Oliver Goldsmith was born on November 10, 1728, in County Longford, Ireland, and became one of the most versatile writers of the eighteenth century, producing the novel The Vicar of Wakefield, the play She Stoops to Conquer, and poems including The Deserted Village (1770), his most celebrated work. He died in London in 1774.
