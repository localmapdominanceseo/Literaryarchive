Scots Wha Hae

by Robert Burns

Written 1793 Published 1794 English Romanticism Public Domain

Published 1794.

Quick Answer

Scots Wha Hae is a poem published 1794.

Scots, wha hae wi' Wallace bled,
Scots, wham Bruce has aften led,
Welcome to your gory bed,
Or to victorie!

Now's the day, and now's the hour;
See the front o' battle lour;
See approach proud Edward's power—
Chains and slaverie!

Wha will be a traitor knave?
Wha can fill a coward's grave?
Wha sae base as be a slave?
Let him turn and flee!

By Oppression's woes and pains!
By your Sons in servile chains!
We will drain our dearest veins,
But they shall be free!

Lay the proud Usurpers low!
Tyrants fall in every foe!
Liberty's in every blow!—
Let us do, or die!

Public domain. Published 1794.

About Robert Burns

Robert Burns was born on January 25, 1759, in Alloway, Scotland, and became Scotland's national poet, celebrated for poetry and songs written largely in the Scots dialect. He spent much of his life farming while writing prolifically, and in his later years devoted himself to collecting and adapting traditional Scottish folk songs and ballads, preserving them for future generations. He died in Dumfries in 1796 at age thirty-seven.
