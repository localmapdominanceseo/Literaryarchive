in Just-

by E.E. Cummings

Written 1920 Published 1920 American Modernism Public Domain

Published in 1920, this spring poem describes the balloon man whistling far and wee as children come running from their games — but the balloon man is lame and goat-footed, a Pan figure whose presence suggests something ambivalent beneath the joy of spring.

Quick Answer

"in Just-" is a poem by E.E. Cummings, published in 1920. In just-spring when the world is mud-luscious, the little lame balloon man whistles far and wee, and eddieandbill and bettyandisbel come running. The balloon man is goat-footed — a Pan figure whose presence brings spring's ambivalent energy.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Free verse with Cummings's characteristic run-together names (eddieandbill, bettyandisbel) and fragmented spacing that slows or speeds the reading.

Themes

Spring as both innocent and mythically charged — the goat-footed balloon man is a Pan figure, and his 'far and wee' whistle draws children irresistibly, suggesting both the joy and the danger in the season's energy.

Historical Context

Published in The Dial in 1920 and collected in Tulips and Chimneys (1923), Cummings's first major collection.

About E.E. Cummings

Edward Estlin Cummings was born on October 14, 1894, in Cambridge, Massachusetts, and became one of the most distinctive American poets of the twentieth century, known for his radical experiments with typography, punctuation, capitalization, and syntax. He published his first major collection, Tulips and Chimneys, in 1923, and continued to innovate formally throughout his career. His love poems, particularly 'i carry your heart with me,' are now among the most frequently quoted in American literature. He died in North Conway, New Hampshire, in 1962.
