To Jane: The Recollection

by Percy Bysshe Shelley

Written 1822 Published 1824 English Romanticism Public Domain

Written in 1822 and published posthumously in 1824, this poem recalls a day Shelley and Jane Williams spent walking together through a pine forest near Pisa — a day so perfect it seems more real than present reality.

Quick Answer

"To Jane: The Recollection" is a poem by Shelley, published posthumously in 1824. It recalls a perfect day walking in a pine forest with Jane Williams — the blue sky, the deer, the spirit of delight that filled everything. Now that day is a remembrance only — like the memory of a past happiness — more fair than the present world.

Now the last day of many days,
All beautiful and bright as thou,
The loveliest and the last, is dead,
Rise, Memory, and write its praise!
Up,—to thy wonted work! come, trace
The epitaph of glory fled,—
For now the Earth has changed its face,
A frown is on the Heaven's brow.

We wandered to the Pine Forest
That skirts the Ocean's foam,
The lightest wind was in its nest,
The tempest in its home.
The whispering waves were half asleep,
The clouds were gone to play,
And on the bosom of the deep
The smile of Heaven lay;
It seemed as if the hour were one
Sent from beyond the skies,
Which scattered from above the sun
A light of Paradise.

Public domain. Written 1822, published posthumously 1824.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Seven eight-line stanzas of alternating tetrameter and trimeter, the form creating a sustained, musical meditation.

Themes

The transformation of experience into memory and memory's superiority to the present — the day was perfect because it is past. Shelley's love poems to Jane Williams are among his most tender.

Historical Context

Written in early 1822, one of the last poems before Shelley's death in July 1822. Jane Williams and her husband Edward were close friends of the Shelleys in Italy.

About Percy Bysshe Shelley

Percy Bysshe Shelley was born on August 4, 1792, in Horsham, England, and became one of the major poets of English Romanticism despite controversy during his lifetime over his radical political and religious views. His poetry combines lyrical intensity with sustained engagement with politics, philosophy, and the natural world. He drowned in a boating accident off the coast of Italy in 1822, at the age of twenty-nine.
