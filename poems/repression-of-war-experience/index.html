Everyone Sang

by Siegfried Sassoon

Written 1919 Published 1919 World War I Poetry Public Domain

Published 1919.

Quick Answer

Everyone Sang is a poem published 1919.

Everyone suddenly burst out singing;
And I was filled with such delight
As prisoned birds must find in freedom,
Winging wildly across the white
Orchards and dark-green fields; on—on—and out of sight.

Everyone's voice was suddenly lifted;
And beauty came like the setting sun:
My heart was shaken with tears; and horror
Drifted away... O, but Everyone
Was a bird; and the song was wordless; the singing will never be done.

About Siegfried Sassoon

Siegfried Sassoon was born on September 8, 1886, in Brenchley, Kent, England, and became one of the most important poets of World War I, known for bitter, satirical poems attacking the war's conduct and the complacency of those who sent young men to die. He served on the Western Front, won the Military Cross, and then publicly refused to return to duty in 1917 — a protest that was handled by sending him to Craiglockhart War Hospital, where he met Wilfred Owen and shaped his poetry. He died in 1967.
