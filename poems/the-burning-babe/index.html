The Burning Babe

by Robert Southwell

Written 1595 Published 1595 English Renaissance Public Domain

Published in 1595, this remarkable poem by the Jesuit martyr Robert Southwell describes a vision of the Christ child burning in a fire of love, his tears making a bath to wash men's souls, his sighs the smoke of his sacrifice.

Quick Answer

"The Burning Babe" is a poem by Robert Southwell, published in 1595. On a frosty Christmas night the speaker sees a burning babe in the air, scorched with excessive heat. The child explains that love is his fire, his fuel is mercy, his furnace is the human heart. The speaker realizes it is Christ and wonders at his sight.

As I in hoary winter's night stood shivering in the snow,
Surprised I was with sudden heat which made my heart to glow;
And lifting up a fearful eye to view what fire was near,
A pretty Babe all burning bright did in the air appear;
Who, scorched with excessive heat, such floods of tears did shed,
As though his floods should quench his flames which with his tears were fed.
'Alas!' quoth he, 'but newly born, in fiery heats I fry,
Yet none approach to warm their hearts or feel my fire but I!
My faultless breast the furnace is, the fuel wounding thorns,
Love is the fire, and sighs the smoke, the ashes shame and scorns;
The fuel Justice layeth on, and Mercy blows the coals,
The metal in this furnace wrought are men's defiled souls,
For which, as now on fire I am to work them to their good,
So will I melt into a bath to wash them in my blood.'
With this he vanished out of sight and swiftly shrunk away,
And straight I called unto mind that it was Christmas Day.

Public domain. Published 1595.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Eighteen lines of iambic heptameter (fourteeners) rhyming in couplets, the long lines giving the poem a dramatic, breathless quality.

Themes

The Incarnation as an act of burning love — Christ's birth is presented as a paradox of cold night and burning fire, of a child who is simultaneously a furnace of divine love. The poem's conceit is sustained and developed with considerable ingenuity.

Historical Context

Published in 1595 after Southwell's execution. Southwell was a Jesuit priest executed for his faith in 1595. Ben Jonson reportedly said he would destroy many of his poems if he could have written The Burning Babe.

About Robert Southwell

Robert Southwell was born around 1561 in Norfolk, England, and became a Jesuit priest who secretly ministered to English Catholics during a period of intense persecution. He was arrested in 1592, tortured, and executed in 1595. His poems, including 'The Burning Babe,' were published the same year as his death and were admired by contemporaries including Ben Jonson. He was canonized as a saint in 1970.
