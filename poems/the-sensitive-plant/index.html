The Sensitive Plant (Extract)

by Percy Bysshe Shelley

Written 1820 Published 1820 English Romanticism Public Domain

Published in 1820, The Sensitive Plant is an allegory of a garden tended by a beautiful Lady whose death brings the garden to ruin. The sensitive plant (mimosa), which more than all things needed tenderness, is the poem's central figure.

Quick Answer

"The Sensitive Plant" is a poem by Shelley, published in 1820. A garden is tended by a Lady who is all love and tenderness. The sensitive plant, which has no joy of its own but drinks of the joy of others, thrives in her care. When she dies, the garden falls to weeds and the sensitive plant dies in the winter. But the poem's conclusion asks: what if death itself is a dream?

A Sensitive Plant in a garden grew,
And the young winds fed it with silver dew,
And it opened its fan-like leaves to the light,
And closed them beneath the kisses of Night.

And the Spring arose on the garden fair,
Like the Spirit of Love felt every where;
And each flower and herb on Earth's dark breast
Rose from the dreams of its wintry rest.

But none ever trembled and panted with bliss
In the garden, the field, or the wilderness,
Like a doe in the noontide with love's sweet want,
As the companionless Sensitive Plant.

The snowdrop, and then the violet,
Arose from the ground with warm rain wet,
And their breath was mixed with fresh odour, sent
From the turf, like the voice and the instrument.

Then the pied wind-flowers and the tulip tall,
And narcissi, the fairest among them all,
Who gaze on their eyes in the stream's recess,
Till they die of their own dear loveliness;

Public domain. Published 1820.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Three parts plus a conclusion in four-line stanzas, the poem's narrative structure moving from paradise through loss to philosophical consolation.

Themes

The fragility of beauty and love, and the philosophical question of whether death is real or only a dream. The sensitive plant represents the capacity for love without the capacity to give love — dependent entirely on the Lady's nurturing care.

Historical Context

Published in Prometheus Unbound (1820). The allegory has been read as a meditation on poetry itself — the poet's sensitivity that requires a nurturing environment.

About Percy Bysshe Shelley

Percy Bysshe Shelley was born on August 4, 1792, in Horsham, England, and became one of the major poets of English Romanticism despite controversy during his lifetime over his radical political and religious views. His poetry combines lyrical intensity with sustained engagement with politics, philosophy, and the natural world. He drowned in a boating accident off the coast of Italy in 1822, at the age of twenty-nine.
