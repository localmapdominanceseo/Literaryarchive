Lines to an Indian Air

by Percy Bysshe Shelley

Written 1819 Published 1822 English Romanticism Public Domain

Published in 1822, this short lyric was written to a tune Shelley heard played on the guitar by Jane Williams. The speaker visits his beloved in a dream, sighing for her, and asks her not to let him die on her breast.

Quick Answer

"Lines to an Indian Air" is a lyric by Shelley, published in 1822. I arise from dreams of thee in the first sweet sleep of night, when the winds are breathing low and the stars are burning bright. I arise and steal to thy garden bower and the wandering airs they faint. Oh press it to thine own again and then let it die.

I arise from dreams of thee
In the first sweet sleep of night,
When the winds are breathing low,
And the stars are shining bright
I arise from dreams of thee,
And a spirit in my feet
Hath led me—who knows how?
To thy chamber window, Sweet!

The wandering airs they faint
On the dark, the silent stream—
The Champak odours fail
Like sweet thoughts in a dream;
The nightingale's complaint,
It dies upon her heart;—
As I must on thine,
Oh, belovèd as thou art!

Oh lift me from the grass!
I die! I faint! I fail!
Let thy love in kisses rain
On my lips and eyelids pale.
My cheek is cold and white, alas!
My heart beats loud and fast;—
Oh! press it to thine own again,
Where it will break at last.

Public domain. Published 1822.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Three five-line stanzas in tetrameter and trimeter, one of Shelley's most song-like lyrics.

Themes

Romantic longing and the erotic dream visit — the lover arises from dreams of the beloved and steals to her garden, overwhelmed by desire.

Historical Context

Published in 1822. Written for Jane Williams. Sometimes called 'The Indian Serenade.'

About Percy Bysshe Shelley

Percy Bysshe Shelley was born on August 4, 1792, in Horsham, England, and became one of the major poets of English Romanticism despite controversy during his lifetime over his radical political and religious views. His poetry combines lyrical intensity with sustained engagement with politics, philosophy, and the natural world. He drowned in a boating accident off the coast of Italy in 1822, at the age of twenty-nine.
