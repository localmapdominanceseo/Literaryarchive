Rizpah

by Alfred Lord Tennyson

Written 1880 Published 1880 Victorian Poetry Public Domain

Published in 1880, Rizpah is a dramatic monologue spoken by an old woman who watched her son hanged for highway robbery and spent years secretly gathering his bones from the gibbet — based on the biblical Rizpah who guarded her sons' remains.

Quick Answer

"Rizpah" is a dramatic monologue by Tennyson, published in 1880. An old woman tells of how her son Willy was hanged for robbery, and how she spent years in secret gathering his bones from the gibbet at night to give him Christian burial. The speaker who visits her is a lady come to convert her — but the old woman knows her duty to her own: bone of my bone, flesh of my flesh. I buried him.

Wailing, wailing, wailing, the wind over land and sea—
And Willy's voice in the wind, 'O mother, come out to me.'
Why should he call me to-night, when he knows that I cannot go?
For the doors are shut at the hour, when the children are in their sleep,
And the wheels of the world are roll'd to their evening rest,
And the stars are all asleep, and none but God is awake.

Nay—you can hear it yourself—it is coming—shaking the walls—
Willy—the moon's in a cloud—Good-night. I am going. He calls.
Come now to me, gentle Willy—yes, gentle enough, for you know
I am but a wandering breeze, and you know that the way is long,
And the hill is sharp and the nights are cold, and I dream and I groan,
For Willy to-night is one of the happiest days of my life,
Yet I think that I am the saddest of all things in the world to-night.

Public domain. Published 1880.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Twelve stanzas of six lines each in dramatic monologue form.

Themes

A mother's fierce love defying society's condemnation — the law hanged Willy, but his mother refuses to accept that judgment. The poem raises the question of whether there is any justice that matters more than a mother's love.

Historical Context

Published in 1880 in Ballads and Other Poems. One of Tennyson's most powerful dramatic monologues, rivaling Browning in its psychological depth.

About Alfred Lord Tennyson

Alfred Lord Tennyson was born on August 6, 1809, in Somersby, Lincolnshire, England, and became the dominant poet of the Victorian era, serving as Poet Laureate of the United Kingdom from 1850 until his death in 1892. His work ranges from brief lyrics and dramatic monologues to the epic In Memoriam A.H.H., his long elegy for his close friend Arthur Henry Hallam. He died at Aldworth, Surrey, in 1892.
