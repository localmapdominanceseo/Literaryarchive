A Birthday

by Christina Rossetti

Written 1857 Published 1862 Victorian Poetry Public Domain

Published in 1862, this poem compares the speaker's heart to a singing bird, a watered shoot, and an apple tree laden with fruit — because her love has come to her.

Quick Answer

A Birthday is a poem by Christina Rossetti, published in 1862. The speaker's heart is like a singing bird, like an apple tree bent with fruit, like a rainbow shell in a halcyon sea — because the birthday of her life is come: her love has come to her.

My heart is like a singing bird
Whose nest is in a water'd shoot;
My heart is like an apple-tree
Whose boughs are bent with thickset fruit;
My heart is like a rainbow shell
That paddles in a halcyon sea;
My heart is gladder than all these
Because my love is come to me.

Raise me a dais of silk and down;
Hang it with vair and purple dyes;
Carve it in doves and pomegranates,
And peacocks with a hundred eyes;
Because the birthday of my life
Is come, my love is come to me.

Public domain. Published 1862.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Two eight-line stanzas of alternating tetrameter, the sustained similes of the first stanza resolved in the second.

Themes

The overflowing abundance of love's arrival.

Historical Context

Published in Goblin Market and Other Poems (1862). One of Rossetti's most celebratory poems.

About Christina Rossetti

Christina Rossetti was born on December 5, 1830, in London, the youngest of the four Rossetti children. She is the most admired Victorian woman poet, known for her lyrical grace, her devout Anglican faith, and her poems of love, death, and longing. Her first major collection Goblin Market and Other Poems (1862) was immediately celebrated. Her brother Dante Gabriel Rossetti was a central figure in the Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood. She died in London in 1894.
