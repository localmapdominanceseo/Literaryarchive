Ode on a Distant Prospect of Eton College (Extract)

by Thomas Gray

Written 1742 Published 1747 English Romanticism Public Domain

Published 1747.

Quick Answer

Ode on a Distant Prospect of Eton College (Extract) is a poem published 1747.

Ye distant spires, ye antique towers,
That crown the watery glade,
Where grateful Science still adores
Her Henry's holy shade;
And ye, that from the stately brow
Of Windsor's heights th' expanse below
Of grove, of lawn, of mead survey,
Whose turf, whose shade, whose flowers among
Wanders the hoary Thames along
His silver-winding way.

Alas, regardless of their doom,
The little victims play!
No sense have they of ills to come,
Nor care beyond to-day:
Yet see how all around 'em wait
The ministers of human fate,
And black Misfortune's baleful train!
Ah, show them where in ambush stand
To seize their prey the murderous band!
Ah, tell them, they are men!

About Thomas Gray

Thomas Gray was born on December 26, 1716, in London, England, and spent most of his adult life as a scholar at Cambridge University. Though he published very little poetry, his 'Elegy Written in a Country Churchyard' (1751) became one of the most widely read and imitated poems in the English language. He died in Cambridge in 1771.
