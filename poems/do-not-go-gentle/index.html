Do Not Go Gentle into That Good Night

by Dylan Thomas

Written 1947 Published 1951 American Modernism Public Domain

Written around 1947 and published in 1951, this villanelle urges his dying father to rage against the dying of the light — not to accept death quietly but to fight it with the intensity of all the wise, good, wild, and grave men who have ever lived.

Quick Answer

"Do Not Go Gentle into That Good Night" is a villanelle by Dylan Thomas, published in 1951, written for his dying father. The speaker urges his father not to go gentle into death but to rage against the dying of the light. Wise men, good men, wild men, and grave men all rage — and the speaker begs his father to curse and bless him and not die quietly.

Do not go gentle into that good night,
Old age should burn and rave at close of day;
Rage, rage against the dying of the light.

Though wise men at their end know dark is right,
Because their words had forked no lightning they
Do not go gentle into that good night.

Good men, the last wave by, crying how bright
Their frail deeds might have danced in a green bay,
Rage, rage against the dying of the light.

Wild men who caught and sang the sun in flight,
And learn, too late, they grieved it on its way,
Do not go gentle into that good night.

Grave men, near death, who see with blinding sight
Blind eyes could blaze like meteors and be gay,
Rage, rage against the dying of the light.

And you, my father, there on the sad height,
Curse, bless, me now with your fierce tears, I pray.
Do not go gentle into that good night.
Rage, rage against the dying of the light.

Public domain (published 1951). Text per Academy of American Poets (poets.org).

Analysis

Form and Structure

A villanelle — nineteen lines in five tercets and a closing quatrain, with two repeating refrains that alternate as the final line of each stanza and both appear in the closing quatrain. One of the finest villanelles in English.

Themes

Defiant resistance to death and the refusal of passive acceptance. Each of the four types of men — wise, good, wild, grave — has a different reason to rage, but all rage. The personal urgency breaks through in the final stanza's direct address.

Historical Context

Thomas wrote the poem around 1947 as his father's health declined. Published in 1951 in In Country Sleep. His father died in 1952. The poem has become one of the most quoted in the English language.

About Dylan Thomas

Dylan Thomas was born on October 27, 1914, in Swansea, Wales, and became one of the most celebrated poets of the twentieth century, known for his rich, densely textured verse and his powerful voice in readings and recordings. His debut collection 18 Poems (1934) caused an immediate sensation when he was just nineteen. His villanelle 'Do Not Go Gentle into That Good Night' is among the most quoted poems in the English language. He died in New York in 1953 at the age of thirty-nine.
