Is My Team Ploughing

by A.E. Housman

Written 1896 Published 1896 Victorian Poetry Public Domain

Published in 1896 in A Shropshire Lad, this dialogue poem has a dead man asking his living friend if things still go on as they did — the team ploughing, the football being kicked, the girl he used to hold — and the friend answering yes to each, until we realize the friend is now with the girl.

Quick Answer

Is My Team Ploughing is a poem by A.E. Housman, published in 1896. A dead man asks: is my team ploughing? The friend answers yes. Is the football being kicked? Yes. Is my girl happy? She lies quiet beside her friends. Is my friend steady and still? He lies quiet — quiet, sleeping, but not with her.

'Is my team ploughing,
That I was used to drive
And hear the harness jingle
When I was man alive?'

Ay, the horses trample,
The harness jingles now;
No change though you lie under
The land you used to plough.

'Is football playing
Along the river shore,
With lads to chase the leather,
Now I stand up no more?'

Ay, the ball is flying,
The lads play heart and soul;
The goal stands up, the keeper
Stands up to keep the goal.

'Is my girl happy,
That I thought hard to leave,
And has she tired of weeping
As she lies down at eve?'

Ay, she lies down lightly,
She lies not down to weep:
Your girl is well contented.
Be still, my lad, and sleep.

'Is my friend hearty,
Now I am thin and pine,
And has he found to sleep in
A better bed than mine?'

Yes, lad, I lie easy,
I lie as lads would choose;
I cheer a dead man's sweetheart,
Never ask me whose.

Public domain. Published 1896 in A Shropshire Lad.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Eight four-line stanzas of alternating question and answer in iambic tetrameter and trimeter, ABAB rhyme.

Themes

Life continuing after death and the living taking the places of the dead — including the dead man's sweetheart. The poem's final revelation is quiet and devastating.

Historical Context

Published in A Shropshire Lad (1896), poem XXVII. One of Housman's finest dramatic poems.

About A.E. Housman

Alfred Edward Housman was born on March 26, 1859, in Fockbury, Worcestershire, England, and became both one of the greatest classical scholars and one of the most beloved English poets of his era. A Shropshire Lad (1896), published at his own expense after being rejected by publishers, became one of the most popular poetry collections ever published in English. He died in Cambridge in 1936.
