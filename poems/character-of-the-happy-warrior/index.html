Character of the Happy Warrior

by William Wordsworth

Written 1806 Published 1807 English Romanticism Public Domain

Published in 1807 in Poems in Two Volumes, this poem describes the ideal military man — who is happy in what he does because it matches what he is, who makes his moral being his prime care, who turns his necessities to glorious gain.

Quick Answer

Character of the Happy Warrior is a poem by Wordsworth, published in 1807. Who is the happy Warrior? He who upon a loud summons makes his soul the mark and aim of all his toil. He who flattened to the unalterable law of life, finds comfort in himself and in his cause. He is the man who is happy in the doing of what he must do.

Who is the happy Warrior? Who is he
That every man in arms should wish to be?
—It is the generous Spirit, who, when brought
Among the tasks of real life, hath wrought
Upon the plan that pleased his boyish thought:
Whose high endeavours are an inward light
That makes the path before him always bright:
Who, with a natural instinct to discern
What knowledge can perform, is diligent to learn;
Abides by this resolve, and stops not there,
But makes his moral being his prime care;
Who, doomed to go in company with Pain,
And Fear, and Bloodshed, miserable train!
Turns his necessity to glorious gain;
In face of these doth exercise a power
Which is our human nature's highest dower.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Sixty-five lines of rhyming couplets in iambic pentameter, the poem's length and rhetorical structure resembling a conduct manual in verse.

Themes

The ideal of virtue as its own reward — the happy warrior is happy not because he wins but because he acts rightly regardless of outcome.

Historical Context

Published in Poems in Two Volumes (1807). Written partly in response to the death of Lord Nelson at Trafalgar (1805).

About William Wordsworth

William Wordsworth was born on April 7, 1770, in Cockermouth, England. Alongside Samuel Taylor Coleridge, he helped launch the English Romantic movement with the 1798 publication of Lyrical Ballads. Wordsworth's poetry championed plain language, the beauty of the natural world, and the inner life of ordinary people. He served as England's Poet Laureate from 1843 until his death in 1850, and his long autobiographical poem The Prelude is considered one of the central achievements of English Romanticism.
