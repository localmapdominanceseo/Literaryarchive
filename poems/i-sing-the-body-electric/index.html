I Sing the Body Electric (Extract)

by Walt Whitman

Written 1855 Published 1855 American Romanticism Public Domain

Published 1855.

Quick Answer

I Sing the Body Electric (Extract) is a poem by its author, published 1855.

I sing the body electric,
The armies of those I love engirth me and I engirth them,
They will not let me off till I go with them, respond to them,
And discorrupt them, and charge them full with the charge of the soul.

Was it doubted that those who corrupt their own bodies conceal themselves?
And if those who defile the living are as bad as they who defile the dead?
And if the body does not do fully as much as the soul?
And if the body were not the soul, what is the soul?

Public domain. Published 1855.

About Walt Whitman

Walt Whitman was born on May 31, 1819, on Long Island, New York. His landmark collection Leaves of Grass, first published in 1855 and expanded throughout his life, broke from traditional verse forms in favor of free verse and sweeping, democratic subject matter. Whitman served as a nurse during the American Civil War, an experience that shaped much of his later work, including his elegy for Abraham Lincoln. He is widely regarded, alongside Emily Dickinson, as one of the founders of a distinctly American poetic voice.
