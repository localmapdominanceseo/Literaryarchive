The Soul Selects Her Own Society

by Emily Dickinson

Written 1862 Published 1890 American Romanticism Public Domain

Written around 1862 and published posthumously in 1890, this poem describes the soul's absolute autonomy — it chooses its own society and then shuts the door, unmoved even by emperors kneeling on its mat or chariots pausing at its gate.

Quick Answer

"The Soul Selects Her Own Society" is a poem by Emily Dickinson, written around 1862. The soul selects its own society and then shuts the door. Even an emperor kneeling on its mat or a chariot pausing at its gate does not move it. From an ample nation it chooses one, and closes the valves of its attention like stone.

The Soul selects her own Society—
Then—shuts the Door—
To her divine Majority—
Present no more—

Unmoved—she notes the Chariots—pausing—
At her low Gate—
Unmoved—an Emperor be kneeling
Upon her Mat—

I've known her—from an ample nation—
Choose One—
Then—close the Valves of her attention—
Like Stone—

Public domain. Text per Academy of American Poets (poets.org).

Analysis

Form and Structure

Three quatrains in common meter with slant rhyme.

Themes

The soul's radical self-sufficiency and its right to choose its own company — even royalty cannot compel its attention. The closing image of stone valves is among the most striking of Dickinson's metaphors for self-enclosure.

Historical Context

Published posthumously in 1890. One of Dickinson's most frequently taught poems about interior freedom and self-determination.

About Emily Dickinson

Emily Dickinson was born on December 10, 1830, in Amherst, Massachusetts, and spent nearly her entire life there. Though she wrote nearly 1,800 poems, fewer than a dozen were published during her lifetime. Her work, marked by unconventional capitalization, slant rhyme, and dashes, was largely discovered and published after her death in 1886. She is now considered one of the most important and original voices in American poetry, known for her meditations on death, immortality, nature, and the inner life.
