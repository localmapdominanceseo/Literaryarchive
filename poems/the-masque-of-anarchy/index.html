The Masque of Anarchy (Extract)

by Percy Bysshe Shelley

Written 1819 Published 1832 English Romanticism Public Domain

Written in 1819 in response to the Peterloo Massacre and published posthumously in 1832, The Masque of Anarchy is Shelley's political poem calling for non-violent resistance to tyranny. The famous passage defines freedom and declares: Rise like lions after slumber.

Quick Answer

The Masque of Anarchy is a political poem by Shelley, written in 1819 after the Peterloo Massacre and published posthumously in 1832. The famous closing stanzas call on the oppressed masses to rise like lions — but non-violently. Shake your chains to earth like dew which in sleep had fallen on you: ye are many, they are few.

As I lay asleep in Italy
There came a voice from over the Sea,
And with great power it forth led me
To walk in the visions of Poesy.

I met Murder on the way—
He had a mask like Castlereagh—
Very smooth he looked, yet grim;
Seven blood-hounds followed him:

All were fat; and well they might
Be in admirable plight,
For one by one, and two by two,
He tossed them human hearts to chew
Which from his wide cloak he drew.

Rise like Lions after slumber
In unvanquishable number,
Shake your chains to earth like dew
Which in sleep had fallen on you—
Ye are many—they are few.

Public domain. Written 1819, published posthumously 1832.

Analysis

Form and Structure

A long poem in four-line ballad stanzas ABAB, accessible and rhetorical, designed to be read by working people.

Themes

Non-violent resistance to political tyranny — Shelley argues that the oppressed should stand together and let tyranny destroy itself on their passive resistance. The famous 'Ye are many, they are few' became an anthem of reform movements.

Historical Context

Written in Italy after news of the Peterloo Massacre (August 16, 1819) reached Shelley. Published posthumously in 1832. Leigh Hunt, who had the manuscript, held it back for years fearing prosecution.

About Percy Bysshe Shelley

Percy Bysshe Shelley was born on August 4, 1792, in Horsham, England, and became one of the major poets of English Romanticism despite controversy during his lifetime over his radical political and religious views. His poetry combines lyrical intensity with sustained engagement with politics, philosophy, and the natural world. He drowned in a boating accident off the coast of Italy in 1822, at the age of twenty-nine.
