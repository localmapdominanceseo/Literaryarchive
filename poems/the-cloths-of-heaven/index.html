He Wishes for the Cloths of Heaven

by W.B. Yeats

Written 1899 Published 1899 Modernism Public Domain

Published in 1899 in The Wind Among the Reeds, this eight-line poem offers the beloved the speaker's dreams as a substitute for the golden and silver cloths of heaven he cannot afford — and asks her to tread softly because she treads on his dreams.

Quick Answer

"He Wishes for the Cloths of Heaven" is a poem by W.B. Yeats, published in 1899. If the speaker had the golden and silver cloths of heaven, he would spread them under his beloved's feet — but being poor, he can only spread his dreams. He asks her to tread softly, for she treads on his dreams.

Had I the heavens' embroidered cloths,
Enwrought with golden and silver light,
The blue and the dim and the dark cloths
Of night and light and the half light,
I would spread the cloths under your feet:
But I, being poor, have only my dreams;
I have spread my dreams under your feet;
Tread softly because you tread on my dreams.

Public domain (published 1899). Text per Academy of American Poets (poets.org).

Analysis

Form and Structure

Eight lines of loose iambic tetrameter, the simplicity of the form giving the final image its force.

Themes

The vulnerability of the lover who can only offer his innermost self — his dreams — to the beloved, and the fear that she will trample them without knowing.

Historical Context

Published in The Wind Among the Reeds (1899), addressed to Maud Gonne. The final line — 'Tread softly because you tread on my dreams' — became one of the most quoted lines in Yeats.

About W.B. Yeats

William Butler Yeats was born on June 13, 1865, near Dublin, Ireland, and became one of the most significant poets of the twentieth century and a central figure in the Irish Literary Revival. His poetry moved from early lyrical, mystically influenced verse toward a sparer, more powerful style concerned with history, aging, and Irish politics. Yeats won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1923 and served as a senator in the Irish Free State. He died in France in 1939.
