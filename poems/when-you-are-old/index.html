When You Are Old

by W.B. Yeats

Written 1891 Published 1892 Modernism Public Domain

Written in 1891 and published in 1892 in The Countess Kathleen and Various Legends and Lyrics, this poem is addressed to Maud Gonne, the Irish nationalist and actress whom Yeats loved for decades, imagining her in old age reading the poem and regretting that she did not return his love.

Quick Answer

"When You Are Old" is a poem by W.B. Yeats, published in 1892. The speaker asks his beloved to imagine herself old and grey, taking down this book and reading it, and remembering how many men loved her beauty — but only one man loved the pilgrim soul in her. He imagines her alone by the fire, murmuring of love's passing, while it has fled to the mountains and hidden its face.

When you are old and grey and full of sleep,
And nodding by the fire, take down this book,
And slowly read, and dream of the soft look
Your eyes had once, and of their shadows deep;

How many loved your moments of glad grace,
And loved your beauty with love false or true,
But one man loved the pilgrim soul in you,
And loved the sorrows of your changing face;

And bending down beside the glowing bars,
Murmur, a little sadly, how Love fled
And paced upon the mountains overhead
And hid his face amid a crowd of stars.

Public domain (published 1892). Text per the Academy of American Poets (poets.org).

Analysis

Form and Structure

Three quatrains of loose iambic pentameter with ABBA rhyme scheme, a gentle, elegiac structure suited to its subject of anticipated regret.

Themes

Unrequited love and the anticipated regret of the beloved who did not return it. The poem distinguishes between those who loved Maud Gonne's beauty — many — and the one man who loved 'the pilgrim soul in her' (Yeats himself). The regret imagined is hers, not his.

Historical Context

Yeats wrote the poem in October 1891, based on a sonnet by Pierre de Ronsard addressed to Hélène de Surgères. He was deeply in love with Maud Gonne, who repeatedly refused his proposals. The poem was published in 1892 and is now one of his most frequently quoted early works.

About W.B. Yeats

William Butler Yeats was born on June 13, 1865, near Dublin, Ireland, and became one of the most significant poets of the twentieth century and a central figure in the Irish Literary Revival. His poetry moved from early lyrical, mystically influenced verse toward a sparer, more powerful style concerned with history, aging, and Irish politics. Yeats won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1923 and served as a senator in the Irish Free State. He died in France in 1939.
