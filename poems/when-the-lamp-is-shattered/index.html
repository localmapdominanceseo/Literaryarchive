When the Lamp Is Shattered

by Percy Bysshe Shelley

Written 1822 Published 1824 English Romanticism Public Domain

Written in 1822 and published posthumously in 1824, this lyric meditates on what happens when the lamp of love is shattered or the rainbow has faded — the light in the dust lies dead, but the heart still survives.

Quick Answer

"When the Lamp Is Shattered" is a poem by Shelley, published posthumously in 1824. When the lamp is shattered, the light lies in the dust. When the cloud is scattered, the rainbow's glory is shed. When the lute is broken, the music still lingers — but when the heart's love is lost, the weak one is singled out to endure what the strong one declines to bear.

When the lamp is shattered
The light in the dust lies dead—
When the cloud is scattered
The rainbow's glory is shed.
When the lute is broken,
Sweet tones are remembered not;
When the lips have spoken,
Loved accents are soon forgot.

As music and splendour
Survive not the lamp and the lute,
The heart's echoes render
No song when the spirit is mute:—
No song but sad dirges,
Like the wind through a ruined cell,
Or the mournful surges
That ring the dead seaman's knell.

When hearts have once mingled
Love first leaves the well-built nest;
The weak one is singled
To endure what it once possessed.
O Love! who bewailest
The frailty of all things here,
Why choose you the frailest
For your cradle, your home, and your bier?

Its passions will rock thee
As the storms rock the ravens on high;
Bright reason will mock thee,
Like the sun from a wintry sky.
From thy nest every rafter
Will rot, and thine eagle home
Leave thee naked to laughter,
When leaves fall and cold winds come.

Public domain. Written 1822, published posthumously 1824.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Four six-line stanzas of alternating tetrameter and trimeter, the poem's regularity contrasting with its sad subject.

Themes

The survival of heartbreak — the images of shattered lamp, faded rainbow, broken lute all describe the loss of love, and the final stanza argues that the weak heart must bear what strong passion cannot.

Historical Context

Written in 1822, shortly before Shelley's death. Published posthumously in 1824. One of his most musical short lyrics.

About Percy Bysshe Shelley

Percy Bysshe Shelley was born on August 4, 1792, in Horsham, England, and became one of the major poets of English Romanticism despite controversy during his lifetime over his radical political and religious views. His poetry combines lyrical intensity with sustained engagement with politics, philosophy, and the natural world. He drowned in a boating accident off the coast of Italy in 1822, at the age of twenty-nine.
