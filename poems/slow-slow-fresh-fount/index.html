Slow, Slow, Fresh Fount

by Ben Jonson

Written 1600 Published 1600 English Renaissance Public Domain

Published in 1600 in Cynthia's Revels, this song is sung by Echo mourning Narcissus — slow, slow, fresh fount, keep time with my salt tears; yet slower yet, oh faintly gentle springs.

Quick Answer

"Slow, Slow, Fresh Fount" is a song by Ben Jonson from Cynthia's Revels (1600). Echo sings of her grief for Narcissus — asking the fount to slow its flow to keep time with her tears, the sighs drop, drop, drop, and the winter weather approaches.

Slow, slow, fresh fount, keep time with my salt tears;
Yet slower, yet; O faintly, gentle springs;
List to the heavy part the music bears,
Woe weeps out her division, when she sings.
Droop herbs and flowers,
Fall grief in showers,
Our beauties are not ours;
O, I could still,
Like melting snow upon some craggy hill,
Drop, drop, drop, drop,
Since nature's pride is now a withered daffodil.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Twelve lines of varying length with careful sound patterns — the slow movement of the lines enacting the slow, reluctant movement of the spring.

Themes

Grief for lost love, figured through the myth of Echo who loved Narcissus in vain. The poem is one of the most musical in Jonson.

Historical Context

From Cynthia's Revels (1600). One of Jonson's finest lyrics for its control of sound and movement.

About Ben Jonson

Ben Jonson was born on June 11, 1572, in London, and became the foremost dramatist and poet of the Jacobean era, second only to Shakespeare in contemporary reputation. His plays Volpone (1606) and The Alchemist (1610) are among the greatest comedies in English. As a poet, his Epigrams, The Forest, and Under-wood collections display unrivaled technical mastery and classical learning. He died in London in 1637.
