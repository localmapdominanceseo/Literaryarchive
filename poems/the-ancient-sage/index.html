Ancient Sage (Extract)

by Alfred Lord Tennyson

Written 1885 Published 1885 Victorian Poetry Public Domain

Published in 1885, The Ancient Sage is a philosophical dialogue between an old sage and a younger skeptic — the sage arguing for the reality of spiritual experience against the skeptic's materialism.

Quick Answer

The Ancient Sage (1885) is Tennyson's dialogue between an aged philosopher and a young skeptic. The sage argues: thou canst not prove the Nameless, O my son, nor canst thou prove the world thou movest in. Thou canst not prove that thou art body alone, nor canst thou prove that thou art spirit alone. But if the Nameless should withdraw from all thy craving, there would follow an absolute blank.

Thou canst not prove the Nameless, O my son,
Nor canst thou prove the world thou movest in,
Thou canst not prove that thou art body alone,
Nor canst thou prove that thou art spirit alone,
Nor canst thou prove that thou art both in one:
Thou canst not prove thou art immortal, no
Nor yet that thou art mortal—nay, my son,
Thou canst not prove that I, who speak with thee,
Am not thyself in converse with thyself,
For nothing worthy proving can be proven,
Nor yet disproven: wherefore thou be wise,
Cleave ever to the sunnier side of doubt,
And cling to Faith beyond the forms of Faith!
She reels not in the storm of warring words,
She brightens at the clash of 'Yes' and 'No,'
She sees the Best that glimmers thro' the Worst,
She feels the Sun is hid but for a night,
She spies the summer thro' the winter bud,
She tastes the fruit before the blossom falls,
She hears the lark within the songless egg,
She finds the fountain where they wail'd 'Mirage!'

Analysis

Form and Structure

Two hundred and eighty-four lines of blank verse.

Themes

The limits of materialism and the reality of spiritual experience — the sage argues that we cannot prove either matter or spirit definitively, but the mystical moments of 'the Nameless' are real experiences.

Historical Context

Published in 1885. One of Tennyson's most extended philosophical poems, drawing on his own mystical experiences.

About Alfred Lord Tennyson

Alfred Lord Tennyson was born on August 6, 1809, in Somersby, Lincolnshire, England, and became the dominant poet of the Victorian era, serving as Poet Laureate of the United Kingdom from 1850 until his death in 1892. His work ranges from brief lyrics and dramatic monologues to the epic In Memoriam A.H.H., his long elegy for his close friend Arthur Henry Hallam. He died at Aldworth, Surrey, in 1892.
