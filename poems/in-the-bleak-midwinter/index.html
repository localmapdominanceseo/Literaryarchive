In the Bleak Midwinter

by Christina Rossetti

Written 1872 Published 1872 Victorian Poetry Public Domain

Published in 1872, this Christmas poem describes the bleak midwinter of the Nativity — frost and snow — and asks what the speaker can give to the Christ child. Her heart.

Quick Answer

In the Bleak Midwinter is a Christmas poem by Christina Rossetti, published in 1872. Frosty wind made moan, earth stood hard as iron. What can I give Him? Give my heart.

In the bleak mid-winter
Frosty wind made moan,
Earth stood hard as iron,
Water like a stone;
Snow had fallen, snow on snow,
Snow on snow,
In the bleak mid-winter
Long ago.

What can I give Him,
Poor as I am?
If I were a shepherd
I would bring a lamb;
If I were a wise man
I would do my part;
Yet what I can I give Him
Give my heart.

Public domain. Published 1872.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Five four-line stanzas of alternating tetrameter and trimeter with ABCB rhyme, hymn-like.

Themes

The Nativity in an English winter landscape, and the question of what the ordinary person can offer the divine.

Historical Context

Published in Scribner's Monthly in January 1872. Set to music by Harold Darke and Gustav Holst.

About Christina Rossetti

Christina Rossetti was born on December 5, 1830, in London, the youngest of the four Rossetti children. She is the most admired Victorian woman poet, known for her lyrical grace, her devout Anglican faith, and her poems of love, death, and longing. Her first major collection Goblin Market and Other Poems (1862) was immediately celebrated. Her brother Dante Gabriel Rossetti was a central figure in the Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood. She died in London in 1894.
