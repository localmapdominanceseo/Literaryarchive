Spring View

by Du Fu

Written 757 Published 757 Tang Dynasty Poetry Public Domain

Written in 757 CE during the An Lushan Rebellion while Du Fu was a prisoner in the captured capital Chang'an, this poem describes a city broken by war while nature continues indifferently — flowers weep at the political situation, birds startle the heart with their song.

Quick Answer

"Spring View" is a poem by Du Fu, written in 757 CE. The nation is broken but mountains and rivers remain. Spring comes to the city and the grass grows deep in the empty streets. Flowers weep at the separation from loved ones; birds startle the heart with their song. The speaker's white hair has grown too thin to hold a hairpin.

The nation broken, mountains and rivers remain;
Spring comes to the city, grass and trees grow deep.
Feeling the times, flowers weep tears;
Hating the separation, birds startle the heart.
Beacon fires for three months running—
A letter from home worth ten thousand gold.
White hair scratched even thinner,
Hardly enough to hold a hairpin.

Public domain translation. Original text 757 CE by Du Fu (712-770 CE). Translation is a free rendering in the public domain.

Analysis

Form and Structure

A regulated verse (lushi) in five-character lines, one of the strictest classical Chinese poetic forms, with tonal patterns and parallel couplets in the middle stanzas.

Themes

The contrast between the permanence of nature and the devastation of war and political collapse. The flowers weeping and the birds startling represent a projection of the speaker's grief onto nature — a form of pathetic fallacy that is a recognized device in classical Chinese poetry.

Historical Context

Du Fu (712-770 CE) is considered one of the two greatest poets of the Tang Dynasty (alongside Li Bai). He wrote Spring View in 757 while captured by rebel forces during the An Lushan Rebellion, one of the most destructive wars in Chinese history. This translation is in the public domain.

About Du Fu

Du Fu (712-770 CE) is considered one of the greatest poets in the Chinese literary tradition, alongside Li Bai. He wrote during the Tang Dynasty and lived through the An Lushan Rebellion, one of the most destructive wars in Chinese history, which profoundly shaped his poetry. He is known for his technical mastery, his moral seriousness, and his compassion for ordinary people suffering under war and famine.
