Because I Liked You Better

by A.E. Housman

Written 1922 Published 1922 Victorian Poetry Public Domain

Published posthumously in More Poems (1936), this poem was written around 1922 and describes the speaker parting from a friend he loved but never told — because telling would have ruined the friendship.

Quick Answer

Because I Liked You Better is a poem by A.E. Housman, published posthumously in 1936. Because I liked you better than suits a man to say, I carried the old secret I gave but not away. My heart is spent and done with your trouble or your joy — we came in and now you go, goodbye to you, my boy.

Because I liked you better
Than suits a man to say,
It irked you, and I promised
To throw the thought away.

To put the world between us
We parted, stiff and dry;
'Good-bye,' said you, 'forget me.'
'I will, no fear,' said I.

If here, where clover whitens
The dead man's knoll, you pass,
And no tall flower to meet you
Stands up from the free grass;

Hold you a spell in passing,
Remember there how men,
Who spent their hours at Ludlow,
Are quiet now and then.

Public domain. Published posthumously 1936.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Four four-line stanzas of alternating iambic tetrameter and trimeter.

Themes

Unexpressed love between men — Housman was homosexual and could not speak of it openly. The poem's restraint is its subject as well as its method.

Historical Context

Published posthumously in More Poems (1936). One of Housman's most personal and most openly emotional poems.

About A.E. Housman

Alfred Edward Housman was born on March 26, 1859, in Fockbury, Worcestershire, England, and became both one of the greatest classical scholars and one of the most beloved English poets of his era. A Shropshire Lad (1896), published at his own expense after being rejected by publishers, became one of the most popular poetry collections ever published in English, carried in the pockets of thousands of soldiers in World War I. He died in Cambridge in 1936.
