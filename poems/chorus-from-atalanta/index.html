Chorus from Atalanta in Calydon

by Algernon Charles Swinburne

Written 1865 Published 1865 Victorian Poetry Public Domain

Published in 1865, this chorus from Swinburne's verse drama argues that the gods created man for sorrow — before the beginning of years, there came to the making of man time with a gift of tears.

Quick Answer

The Chorus from Atalanta in Calydon by Swinburne (1865) argues the gods made man for suffering. Before the beginning of years there came to the making of man time with a gift of tears, grief with a glass that ran, strength without hands to smite, love that endures for a breath, night the shadow of light, and life the shadow of death.

Before the beginning of years
There came to the making of man
Time, with a gift of tears;
Grief, with a glass that ran;
Pleasure, with pain for leaven;
Summer, with flowers that fell;
Remembrance fallen from heaven,
And madness risen from hell;
Strength without hands to smite;
Love that endures for a breath;
Night, the shadow of light,
And life, the shadow of death.

And the high gods took in hand
Fire, and the falling of tears,
And a measure of sliding sand
From under the feet of the years;
And wrought with weeping and laughter,
And fashioned with loathing and love,
With life before and after
And death beneath and above,
For a day and a night and a morrow,
That his strength might endure for a span
With travail and heavy sorrow,
The holy spirit of man.

Public domain. Published 1865 in Atalanta in Calydon.

Analysis

Form and Structure

A long choral ode in complex anapestic rhythms, Swinburne's most celebrated verse.

Themes

The injustice of divine creation — the gods gave man love and beauty precisely so he might suffer their loss.

Historical Context

Published in Atalanta in Calydon (1865). This play established Swinburne's reputation as a major poet.

About Algernon Charles Swinburne

Algernon Charles Swinburne was born on April 5, 1837, in London, and became one of the most technically accomplished and musically dazzling of all Victorian poets. His Poems and Ballads (1866) caused an immediate scandal for its paganism, eroticism, and attacks on conventional morality. His verse drama Atalanta in Calydon (1865) established his reputation. He died at Putney in 1909.
