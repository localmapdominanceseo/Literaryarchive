The Man He Killed

by Thomas Hardy

Written 1902 Published 1902 Victorian Poetry Public Domain

Published in 1902, this poem is spoken by a soldier who shot a man in battle — a man who, had they met in an inn, might have been his friend. He puzzles over the absurdity of war.

Quick Answer

The Man He Killed is a poem by Thomas Hardy, published in 1902. The speaker shot a man in battle. Had they met in a bar, they would have drunk together. They were both poor fellows who enlisted out of lack of other work. You shoot a fellow and it is quaint and curious war is.

'Had he and I but met
By some old ancient inn,
We should have sat us down to wet
Right many a nipperkin!

'But ranged as infantry,
And staring face to face,
I shot at him as he at me,
And killed him in his place.

'I shot him dead because—
Because he was my foe,
Just so: my foe of course he was;
That's clear enough; although

'He thought he'd 'list, perhaps,
Off-hand like—just as I—
Was out of work—had sold his traps—
No other reason why.

'Yes; quaint and curious war is!
You shoot a fellow down
You'd treat if met where any bar is,
Or help to half-a-crown.'

Public domain. Published 1902.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Five quatrains of alternating iambic tetrameter and trimeter with ABAB rhyme, the plain ballad form suiting the plain soldier's voice.

Themes

The absurdity and waste of war — the soldier cannot find a reason for killing the man beyond the bare fact that he was the enemy, which seems inadequate when he thinks about it.

Historical Context

Published in 1902, during the Second Boer War. Hardy was deeply opposed to the war and the poem is his most direct anti-war statement.

About Thomas Hardy

Thomas Hardy was born on June 2, 1840, in Higher Bockhampton, Dorset, England, and became one of the greatest novelists and poets of the Victorian and Edwardian eras. After Jude the Obscure (1895) was attacked for immorality, he stopped writing fiction and devoted himself entirely to poetry, producing eight volumes. His elegies for his first wife Emma, written after her sudden death in 1912, are considered among the finest in the English language. He died in Dorchester in 1928.
