A Dream Within a Dream

by Edgar Allan Poe

Written 1849 Published 1849 American Gothic Romanticism Public Domain

Published in March 1849, just months before Poe's death, this poem asks whether life itself is real or merely a dream within a dream — whether anything we perceive or love can truly be held, or whether everything slips away like sand through the fingers.

Quick Answer

"A Dream Within a Dream" is a poem by Edgar Allan Poe, published in 1849. The speaker parts from a loved one, arguing that even if all he has seen and hoped has been a dream, it is still real — then stands on a seashore watching grains of sand slip through his fingers and crying out to God: is all that we see or seem just a dream within a dream?

Take this kiss upon the brow!
And, in parting from you now,
Thus much let me avow—
You are not wrong, who deem
That my days have been a dream;
Yet if hope has flown away
In a night, or in a day,
In a vision, or in none,
Is it therefore the less gone?
All that we see or seem
Is but a dream within a dream.

I stand amid the roar
Of a surf-tormented shore,
And I hold within my hand
Grains of the golden sand—
How few! yet how they creep
Through my fingers to the deep,
While I weep—while I weep!
O God! can I not grasp
Them with a tighter clasp?
O God! can I not save
One from the pitiless wave?
Is all that we see or seem
But a dream within a dream?

Analysis

Form and Structure

Two stanzas of iambic tetrameter with irregular rhyme, moving from an intimate farewell in the first stanza to a desolate shore in the second, where the central question becomes cosmic rather than personal.

Themes

The unreality of experience and the impossibility of holding onto anything — love, time, certainty, sanity. The sand slipping through the fingers is a classic memento mori image, but Poe radicalizes it: the question is not just whether beauty fades but whether anything we perceive is real at all.

Historical Context

Poe published the poem in the Boston periodical Flag of Our Union in March 1849, revising it significantly from an 1827 poem called 'Imitation.' It was one of his last publications before his mysterious death in October 1849.

About Edgar Allan Poe

Edgar Allan Poe was born on January 19, 1809, in Boston, Massachusetts. A master of both poetry and short fiction, Poe is credited with helping originate the detective story and significantly shaping the modern horror and gothic genres. His poetry is known for its musicality, melancholy tone, and recurring themes of loss, death, and longing. He died in Baltimore in 1849 under circumstances that remain debated to this day.
