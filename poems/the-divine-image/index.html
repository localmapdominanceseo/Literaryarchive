The Divine Image

by William Blake

Written 1789 Published 1789 English Romanticism Public Domain

Published 1789.

Quick Answer

The Divine Image is a poem published 1789.

To Mercy Pity Peace and Love,
All pray in their distress:
And to these virtues of delight
Return their thankfulness.

For Mercy Pity Peace and Love,
Is God our father dear:
And Mercy Pity Peace and Love,
Is Man his child and care.

For Mercy has a human heart
Pity, a human face:
And Love, the human form divine,
And Peace, the human dress.

Then every man of every clime,
That prays in his distress,
Prays to the human form divine
Love Mercy Pity Peace.

And all must love the human form,
In heathen, turk or jew.
Where Mercy, Love & Pity dwell,
There God is dwelling too.

Public domain. Published 1789.

About William Blake

William Blake was born on November 28, 1757, in London, England, and worked as a poet, painter, and printmaker, producing illustrated volumes of poetry that he engraved, printed, and hand-colored himself. Largely unrecognized during his lifetime, he is now considered one of the founding figures of English Romanticism. His best-known collection, Songs of Innocence and of Experience, pairs poems of childlike wonder with darker counterparts exploring suffering and moral complexity. He died in London in 1827.
