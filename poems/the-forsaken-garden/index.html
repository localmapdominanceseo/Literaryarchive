The Forsaken Garden

by Algernon Charles Swinburne

Written 1876 Published 1876 Victorian Poetry Public Domain

Published in 1876 in Poems and Ballads Second Series, this poem describes an abandoned garden on a cliff above the sea where lovers once walked — now overgrown with weeds, returning to nature, while the sea remains indifferent.

Quick Answer

The Forsaken Garden is a poem by Swinburne, published in 1876. In a coign of the cliff between lowland and highland, at the sea-down's edge between windward and lee, walled round with rocks as an inland island, the ghost of a garden fronts the sea. Lovers once walked here — now all are dead.

In a coign of the cliff between lowland and highland,
At the sea-down's edge between windward and lee,
Walled round with rocks as an inland island,
The ghost of a garden fronts the sea.
A girdle of brushwood and thorn encloses
The steep square slope of the blossomless bed
Where the weeds that grew green from the graves of its roses
Now lie dead.

The fields fall southward, abrupt and broken,
To the low last edge of the long lone land.
If a step should sound or a word be spoken,
Would a ghost not rise at the strange guest's hand?
So long have the grey bare walks lain guestless,
Through branches and briars if a man make way,
He shall find no life but the sea-wind's, restless
Night and day.

Public domain. Published 1876 in Poems and Ballads Second Series.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Eight nine-line stanzas with complex rhyme, the poem's landscape as melancholy as its subject.

Themes

The decay of all human pleasures and loves in the face of indifferent nature — the forsaken garden as a metaphor for all human endeavor.

Historical Context

Published in Poems and Ballads Second Series (1876). One of Swinburne's most admired nature poems.

About Algernon Charles Swinburne

Algernon Charles Swinburne was born on April 5, 1837, in London, and became one of the most technically accomplished and musically dazzling of all Victorian poets. His Poems and Ballads (1866) caused an immediate scandal for its paganism, eroticism, and attacks on conventional morality. His verse drama Atalanta in Calydon (1865) established his reputation. He died at Putney in 1909.
