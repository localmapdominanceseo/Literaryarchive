Stanzas Written in Dejection, Near Naples

by Percy Bysshe Shelley

Written 1818 Published 1824 English Romanticism Public Domain

Written in December 1818 near Naples and published posthumously in 1824, this poem describes Shelley alone by the sea in perfect weather — the sun is warm, the sky is clear, the waves are charming — but he has neither health nor quiet nor hope, and could die happily in such a beautiful place.

Quick Answer

"Stanzas Written in Dejection, Near Naples" is a poem by Shelley, written in 1818 and published posthumously in 1824. The sun is warm, the sea is charming, the city gleams in the noon-day light — but the speaker has no hope, no health, no love, no leisure. He has neither fame, power, nor love to make his life precious. He could lie down like a tired child and weep away the life of care which he has borne too long.

The sun is warm, the sky is clear,
The waves are dancing fast and bright,
Blue isles and snowy mountains wear
The purple noon's transparent might,
The breath of the moist earth is light,
Around its unexpanded buds;
Like many a voice of one delight,
The winds, the birds, the ocean floods,
The City's voice itself, is soft like Solitude's.

I see the Deep's untrampled floor
With green and purple seaweeds strown;
I see the waves upon the shore,
Like light dissolved in star-showers, thrown:
I sit upon the sands alone,—
The lightning of the noontide ocean
Is flashing round me, and a tone
Arises from its measured motion,
How sweet! did any heart now share in my emotion.

Alas! I have nor hope nor health,
Nor peace within nor calm around,
Nor that content surpassing wealth
The sage in meditation found,
And walked with inward glory crowned—
Nor fame, nor power, nor love, nor leisure.
Others I see whom these surround—
Smiling they live, and call life pleasure;—
To me that cup has been dealt in another measure.

Public domain. Written 1818, published posthumously 1824.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Five seven-line stanzas in alternating iambic tetrameter and trimeter with complex rhyme, each stanza ending with a shorter third and sixth line.

Themes

Depression and desolation against a backdrop of natural beauty — the contrast between the perfect day and the speaker's inner emptiness is the poem's central tension. Shelley embraces rather than fights against the idea of death as relief.

Historical Context

Written in December 1818 when Shelley was deeply unhappy in Naples. Published posthumously in 1824.

About Percy Bysshe Shelley

Percy Bysshe Shelley was born on August 4, 1792, in Horsham, England, and became one of the major poets of English Romanticism despite controversy during his lifetime over his radical political and religious views. His poetry combines lyrical intensity with sustained engagement with politics, philosophy, and the natural world. He drowned in a boating accident off the coast of Italy in 1822, at the age of twenty-nine.
