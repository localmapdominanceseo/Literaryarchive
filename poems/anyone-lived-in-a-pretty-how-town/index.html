anyone lived in a pretty how town

by E.E. Cummings

Written 1940 Published 1940 American Modernism Public Domain

Published in 1940 in 50 Poems, this is one of Cummings's most celebrated and most puzzling poems — a love story and a life story told entirely with shifted syntax, where 'anyone' and 'noone' are the lovers, and the seasons mark the passage of their lives.

Quick Answer

"anyone lived in a pretty how town" is a poem by E.E. Cummings, published in 1940. Through constantly shifting syntax and the use of 'anyone' and 'noone' as proper names for the lovers, the poem tells a complete life story: anyone lived, loved noone who loved him, they lived and died, were buried side by side, and the world went on with its seasons indifferently.

anyone lived in a pretty how town
(with up so floating many bells down)
spring summer autumn winter
he sang his didn't he danced his did.

Women and men(both little and small)
cared for anyone not at all
they sowed their isn't they reaped their same
sun moon stars rain

children guessed(but only a few
and down they forgot as up they grew
autumn winter spring summer)
that noone loved him more by more

when by now and tree by leaf
she laughed his joy she cried his grief
bird by snow and stir by still
anyone's any was all to her

someones married their everyones
laughed their cryings and did their dance
(sleep wake hope and then)they
said their nevers they slept their dream

stars rain sun moon
(and only the snow can begin to explain
how children are apt to forget to remember
with up so floating many bells down)

one day anyone died i guess
(and noone stooped to kiss his face)
busy folk buried them side by side
little by little and was by was

all by all and deep by deep
and more by more they dream their sleep
noone and anyone earth by april
wish by spirit and if by yes.

Women and men(both dong and ding)
summer autumn winter spring
reaped their sowing and went their came
sun moon stars rain

Analysis

Form and Structure

Nine four-line stanzas in Cummings's characteristic lowercase style. 'Anyone' is the male lover, 'noone' the female — both are unnamed, universalized. The seasons (spring, summer, autumn, winter) recur as a refrain marking the passage of time.

Themes

Love, community indifference, the passage of time, and mortality — the complete arc of two people's lives told in universalizing terms. The townspeople, called 'someones' and 'everyones,' do not notice or care about anyone and noone's love, yet that love is presented as the realest thing in the poem.

Historical Context

Published in 50 Poems (1940). The poem's syntactic dislocations — adjectives used as nouns, pronouns used as names — are central to its meaning, making 'anyone' simultaneously a specific person and a universal one.

About E.E. Cummings

Edward Estlin Cummings was born on October 14, 1894, in Cambridge, Massachusetts, and became one of the most distinctive American poets of the twentieth century, known for his radical experiments with typography, punctuation, capitalization, and syntax. He published his first major collection, Tulips and Chimneys, in 1923, and continued to innovate formally throughout his career. His love poems, particularly 'i carry your heart with me,' are now among the most frequently quoted in American literature. He died in North Conway, New Hampshire, in 1962.
