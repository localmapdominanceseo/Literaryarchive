I Travelled Among Unknown Men

by William Wordsworth

Written 1801 Published 1807 English Romanticism Public Domain

Published in 1807 in Poems in Two Volumes, this is the last of the Lucy poems — the speaker, having travelled abroad, has returned to England and now associates England itself with his love for Lucy, who lived and died here.

Quick Answer

I Travelled Among Unknown Men is a poem by Wordsworth, published in 1807. The speaker travelled abroad and did not love England until he was away. Now he loves it — for England is the land where Lucy lived, where the wheel she turned is still. In his heart is a work of simple joy that Lucy's hands were last to move.

I travelled among unknown men,
In lands beyond the sea;
Nor, England! did I know till then
What love I bore to thee.

'Tis past, that melancholy dream!
Nor will I quit thy shore
A second time; for still I seem
To love thee more and more.

Among thy mountains did I feel
The joy of my desire;
And she I cherished turned her wheel
Beside an English fire.

Thy mornings showed, thy nights concealed
The bowers where Lucy played;
And thine too is the last green field
That Lucy's eyes surveyed.

Analysis

Form and Structure

Four quatrains of alternating iambic tetrameter and trimeter with ABAB rhyme.

Themes

Love of England filtered through love of the dead Lucy — the nation becomes associated with the beloved through grief.

Historical Context

Published in Poems in Two Volumes (1807). The last of the five Lucy poems, apparently written after the others.

About William Wordsworth

William Wordsworth was born on April 7, 1770, in Cockermouth, England. Alongside Samuel Taylor Coleridge, he helped launch the English Romantic movement with the 1798 publication of Lyrical Ballads. Wordsworth's poetry championed plain language, the beauty of the natural world, and the inner life of ordinary people. He served as England's Poet Laureate from 1843 until his death in 1850, and his long autobiographical poem The Prelude is considered one of the central achievements of English Romanticism.
