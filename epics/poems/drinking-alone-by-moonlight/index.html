Drinking Alone by Moonlight

by Li Bai

Written 740 Published 740 Tang Dynasty Poetry Public Domain

Written around 740 CE, this poem by Li Bai describes drinking alone among flowers under the moon, inviting the moon and his shadow to join him as companions, dancing with them in a trio, though they cannot truly share the pleasure — to meet again among the stars.

Quick Answer

"Drinking Alone by Moonlight" is a poem by Li Bai, written around 740 CE. The speaker drinks alone among flowers, with no one to share the cup. He raises it to invite the bright moon, and with his shadow they become three. The moon does not drink and the shadow only mimics — yet he makes them his companions for dancing, and promises to meet them in the Milky Way.

A cup of wine, under the flowering trees;
I drink alone, for no friend is near.
Raising my cup I beckon the bright moon,
For he, with my shadow, will make three men.
The moon, alas, is no drinker of wine;
Listless, my shadow creeps about at my side.
Yet with the moon as friend and the shadow as slave
I must make merry before the Spring is spent.
To the songs I sing the moon flickers her beams;
In the dance I weave my shadow tangles and breaks.
While we were sober, three shared the fun;
Now we are drunk, each goes his way.
May we long share our odd, inanimate feast,
And meet at last on the Cloudy River of the sky.

Public domain translation by Arthur Waley (1919). Original c.740 CE by Li Bai (701-762 CE).

Analysis

Form and Structure

A yuefu (old-style ballad) in seven-character lines, Li Bai's characteristic expansive voice playing with the solitude and the imagination of company where there is none.

Themes

The solitude of the exceptional person who cannot find worthy companions, resolved through imagination and intoxication — making the moon and his own shadow into friends. The melancholy and the playfulness coexist without resolution.

Historical Context

Li Bai was famous in his own lifetime for his wine-drinking, his friendship with the moon, and his Taoist-influenced celebration of spontaneity. This poem is one of several he wrote on the theme of solitary drinking with the moon as companion.

About Li Bai

Li Bai (701-762 CE), also known as Li Po, is one of the two greatest poets of the Tang Dynasty. He was famous in his own lifetime for his Taoist-influenced celebration of wine, the moon, nature, and friendship. His poetry is known for its imaginative freedom, its musical qualities, and its range from playful to sublime.
