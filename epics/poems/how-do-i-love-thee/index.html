How Do I Love Thee? (Sonnet 43)

by Elizabeth Barrett Browning

Written 1845 Published 1850 Victorian Poetry Public Domain

Written around 1845 and published in 1850 in Sonnets from the Portuguese, this is the most famous of Elizabeth Barrett Browning's sonnet sequence addressed to her husband Robert Browning, counting the ways she loves him — in breadth, depth, and height, by day, by candlelight, in grief, and in faith.

Quick Answer

"How Do I Love Thee?" is a sonnet by Elizabeth Barrett Browning, published in 1850 in Sonnets from the Portuguese. The speaker counts the ways she loves: to the depth and breadth and height her soul can reach, with the passion of old griefs and childhood faith, with all her breath and smiles and tears — and, if God choose, she shall love better after death.

How do I love thee? Let me count the ways.
I love thee to the depth and breadth and height
My soul can reach, when feeling out of sight
For the ends of Being and ideal Grace.
I love thee to the level of every day's
Most quiet need, by sun and candle-light.
I love thee freely, as men strive for Right;
I love thee purely, as they turn from Praise.
I love thee with the passion put to use
In my old griefs, and with my childhood's faith.
I love thee with a love I seemed to lose
With my lost saints. I love thee with the breath,
Smiles, tears, of all my life; and, if God choose,
I shall but love thee better after death.

Public domain. Published 1850 in Sonnets from the Portuguese.

Analysis

Form and Structure

A Petrarchan sonnet in iambic pentameter with the rhyme scheme ABBAABBA CDCDCD. The octave catalogs the dimensions of love spatially; the sestet moves through time and intensity before the immortal close.

Themes

The comprehensive, all-encompassing nature of love — measured in every dimension of soul, feeling, and time. The poem moves from spatial metaphor (depth, breadth, height) to emotional modes (freely, purely, passionately, with childhood faith) to the final claim that love may even intensify after death.

Historical Context

One of forty-four sonnets in Sonnets from the Portuguese (1850), written during Barrett Browning's courtship with and early marriage to Robert Browning. The title 'from the Portuguese' was a playful disguise — Robert called Elizabeth his 'little Portuguese' — to make the personal poems appear to be translations.

About Elizabeth Barrett Browning

Elizabeth Barrett Browning was born on March 6, 1806, in County Durham, England, and became one of the most celebrated poets of the Victorian era. She was largely self-educated and began writing poetry as a child. Her Sonnets from the Portuguese (1850), a sequence of forty-four love sonnets addressed to her husband Robert Browning, are her most enduring work. She also wrote Aurora Leigh (1856), a verse novel advocating for women's independence and artistic life. She died in Florence in 1861.
