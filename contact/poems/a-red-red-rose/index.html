A Red, Red Rose

by Robert Burns

Written 1794 Published 1794 English Romanticism Public Domain

First published in 1794 and written largely in the Scots dialect, this song-poem compares the speaker's love to a freshly bloomed rose and a sweet melody, then vows that love will last until the seas dry up and the rocks melt in the sun.

Quick Answer

"A Red, Red Rose" is a song-poem by Robert Burns, first published in 1794. The speaker compares his love to a newly bloomed rose and a sweet melody, then makes a series of escalating vows of devotion that will last until the seas run dry and the rocks melt in the sun, before parting with a promise to return.

O my Luve's like a red, red rose
That's newly sprung in June;
O my Luve's like the melodie
That's sweetly play'd in tune.

As fair art thou, my bonnie lass,
So deep in luve am I:
And I will luve thee still, my dear,
Till a' the seas gang dry:

Till a' the seas gang dry, my dear,
And the rocks melt wi' the sun:
I will luve thee still, my dear,
While the sands o' life shall run.

And fare thee weel, my only Luve!
And fare thee weel, a while!
And I will come again, my Luve,
Tho' it were ten thousand mile.

Public domain. Text per the Academy of American Poets (poets.org).

Analysis

Form and Structure

Written as a ballad in four quatrains, alternating lines of iambic tetrameter and trimeter, the traditional meter of the ballad form. Burns based the poem on older traditional Scottish songs he collected and adapted, and it was originally intended to be sung rather than read silently.

Themes

Eternal devotion and the tension between love's permanence and time's relentless passage. The poem's escalating impossible vows (seas running dry, rocks melting) assert love's endurance, while the final stanza's farewell and promise to return introduces a note of separation that some readers interpret as undercutting the poem's surface optimism about love lasting forever.

Historical Context

Burns spent the final years of his life collecting and adapting traditional Scottish ballads and folk songs, often reworking fragments he heard sung in the countryside into polished literary verse. 'A Red, Red Rose' was first published, set to music, in Pietro Urbani's 1794 collection A Selection of Scots Songs, and Burns considered it part of his broader project of preserving Scottish oral song traditions.

About Robert Burns

Robert Burns was born on January 25, 1759, in Alloway, Scotland, and became Scotland's national poet, celebrated for poetry and songs written largely in the Scots dialect. He spent much of his life farming while writing prolifically, and in his later years devoted himself to collecting and adapting traditional Scottish folk songs and ballads, preserving them for future generations. He died in Dumfries in 1796 at age thirty-seven.
