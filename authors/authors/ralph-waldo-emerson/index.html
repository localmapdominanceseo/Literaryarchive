Ralph Waldo Emerson

1803–1882 American American Romanticism

Ralph Waldo Emerson was born on May 25, 1803, in Boston, Massachusetts, and became the central figure of American Transcendentalism — the philosophical and literary movement that emphasized the divinity of nature, individual intuition, and the unity of all things. His Essays (1841 and 1844) and his poetry exerted enormous influence on American literature and thought, and his lecture 'The American Scholar' (1837) has been called America's intellectual Declaration of Independence. He died in Concord, Massachusetts, in 1882.
