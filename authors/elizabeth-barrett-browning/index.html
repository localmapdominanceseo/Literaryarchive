Elizabeth Barrett Browning

1806–1861 British Victorian Poetry

Elizabeth Barrett Browning was born on March 6, 1806, in County Durham, England, and became one of the most celebrated poets of the Victorian era. Largely confined to her room by chronic illness, she corresponded with Robert Browning, who eventually persuaded her to elope with him to Italy in 1846. Her Sonnets from the Portuguese (1850), written during their courtship, is one of the great love sequences in English. Aurora Leigh (1856), her verse novel, was enormously influential on later women writers. She died in Florence in 1861.
