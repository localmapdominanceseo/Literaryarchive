Henry Wadsworth Longfellow

1807–1882 American American Romanticism

Henry Wadsworth Longfellow was born on February 27, 1807, in Portland, Maine, and became the most popular and widely read American poet of the nineteenth century. His accessible style, traditional meters, and frequent use of historical and legendary American subject matter, as in 'Paul Revere's Ride' and The Song of Hiawatha, made his work a fixture of American education for generations. He taught modern languages at Harvard for many years and died in Cambridge, Massachusetts, in 1882.
