Christina Rossetti

1830–1894 British Victorian Poetry

Christina Rossetti was born on December 5, 1830, in London, into an artistic family; her brother Dante Gabriel Rossetti was a noted painter and poet associated with the Pre-Raphaelite movement. She began writing poetry as a teenager and became one of the most significant Victorian poets, known for devotional verse, narrative poems such as Goblin Market, and lyrics that quietly subvert Victorian expectations of women. She died in London in 1894.
