Richard Lovelace

1617–1657 British English Renaissance

Richard Lovelace was born on December 9, 1617, in Woolwich, England, and became the most celebrated of the Cavalier poets, known for poems of love, loyalty, and honour written during the English Civil War. He was imprisoned twice for his Royalist sympathies, and wrote his two most famous poems — 'To Lucasta, Going to the Wars' and 'To Althea, from Prison' — during these periods of confinement. He died in poverty in London in 1657.
