Christopher Marlowe

1564–1593 British English Renaissance

Christopher Marlowe was born in February 1564 in Canterbury, England, and became the foremost English playwright before Shakespeare, writing Doctor Faustus, Tamburlaine, and The Jew of Malta. He was also a significant lyric poet. He died in a tavern brawl in Deptford in May 1593 at the age of twenty-nine.
