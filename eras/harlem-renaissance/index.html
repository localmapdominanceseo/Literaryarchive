Harlem Renaissance

The Harlem Renaissance was a flowering of African American literature, art, music, and intellectual life centered in the Harlem neighborhood of New York City during the 1920s. Writers including Langston Hughes, Claude McKay, and Zora Neale Hurston developed new literary forms, often drawing on blues and jazz rhythms, to celebrate Black culture and confront American racism directly.
