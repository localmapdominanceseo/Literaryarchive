American Romanticism

American Romanticism flourished from roughly 1820 to 1865, emphasizing individualism, emotion, the sublime power of nature, and a distinctly American voice distinct from European tradition. Its poets, including Walt Whitman and Emily Dickinson, broke from formal convention to explore democracy, mortality, and the inner self.

Poets in this era: Emily Dickinson · Henry Wadsworth Longfellow · James Russell Lowell · John Greenleaf Whittier · Julia Ward Howe · Oliver Wendell Holmes Sr. · Ralph Waldo Emerson · Walt Whitman · William Cullen Bryant
