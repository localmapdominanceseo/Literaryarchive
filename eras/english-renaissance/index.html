English Renaissance

The English Renaissance, spanning roughly the late fifteenth through early seventeenth centuries, saw an extraordinary flourishing of English poetry and drama, heavily influenced by the rediscovery of classical and Italian literary forms. William Shakespeare's 154 sonnets, written during this period, established the Shakespearean sonnet as one of the defining forms of English-language poetry.

Poets in this era: Alexander Pope · Christopher Marlowe · Edmund Spenser · John Dryden · John Milton · Robert Herrick · Sir Philip Sidney · Sir Walter Raleigh · William Shakespeare
