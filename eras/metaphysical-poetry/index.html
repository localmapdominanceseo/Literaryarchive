Metaphysical Poetry

Metaphysical poetry refers to a loose group of 17th-century English poets, led by John Donne, known for intellectual wit, elaborate extended metaphors called conceits, and a willingness to fuse intense personal or spiritual feeling with rigorous, almost legalistic argument. The term was originally used somewhat dismissively by later critics before being rehabilitated as a description of the group's distinctive intellectual intensity.
