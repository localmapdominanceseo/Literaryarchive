Victorian Poetry

Victorian poetry, spanning the reign of Queen Victoria from 1837 to 1901, often grappled with faith, doubt, social change, and death amid the rapid industrialization and shifting social structures of nineteenth-century Britain. Poets like Christina Rossetti combined formal traditional structures, such as the sonnet, with subject matter that quietly challenged Victorian social expectations.
