Japanese Haiku

The haiku is a Japanese poetic form of seventeen syllables in three lines, traditionally containing a seasonal reference (kigo) and presenting a moment of perception that opens onto a larger reality. Matsuo Bashō elevated it to a serious literary art in the seventeenth century; Kobayashi Issa and Yosa Buson are among the other great masters of the classical tradition.
