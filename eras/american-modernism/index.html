American Modernism

American Modernism, spanning roughly the 1900s through the 1940s, broke from Victorian sentimentality and traditional poetic forms in favor of plain language, fragmented perspectives, and a direct engagement with the realities of modern life. Robert Frost, while more formally traditional than many modernists, shares the movement's focus on ordinary speech and psychological complexity beneath simple surfaces.

Poets in this era: Carl Sandburg · Robert Frost · Wallace Stevens · William Carlos Williams
