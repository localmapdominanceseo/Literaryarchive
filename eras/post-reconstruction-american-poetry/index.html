Post-Reconstruction American Poetry

Poetry written by African American authors in the decades following the end of Reconstruction (1877) and before the Harlem Renaissance, a period marked by the rise of Jim Crow segregation despite formal emancipation. Paul Laurence Dunbar, one of the first African American poets to achieve national recognition, wrote in both standard English and African American dialect verse during this era.
