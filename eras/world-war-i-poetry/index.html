World War I Poetry

Poetry written during or directly about the First World War (1914-1918), ranging from early patriotic verse to the disillusioned, visceral accounts of trench warfare by poets like Wilfred Owen and Siegfried Sassoon. John McCrae's 'In Flanders Fields' established the red poppy as the enduring symbol of wartime remembrance.

Poets in this era: John McCrae · Laurence Binyon · Rupert Brooke · Siegfried Sassoon · Wilfred Owen
