Figurative Language

Simile

A figure of speech that directly compares two unlike things using a connecting word such as 'like' or 'as.'

In Plain English

A simile makes its comparison explicit through a connecting word, which distinguishes it from a metaphor (which states the comparison as if it were literally true, without 'like' or 'as'). Similes are often used to make an abstract feeling or unfamiliar experience concrete by linking it to something the reader already knows well.

Examples

William Wordsworth opens "I Wandered Lonely as a Cloud" with the poem's defining simile, comparing the speaker's solitary wandering to a cloud drifting alone across the sky.
