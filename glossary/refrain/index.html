Sound and Structure

Refrain

A line, phrase, or group of lines that repeats at intervals throughout a poem or song, often at the end of each stanza.

In Plain English

A refrain creates rhythm and emphasis through repetition, often reinforcing the poem's central emotion or idea each time it returns. In elegies and laments specifically, a refrain can function the way a chorus does in music, giving the reader a steady, almost ritual return to the poem's core grief or image.

Examples

Walt Whitman closes each stanza of "O Captain! My Captain!" with the refrain 'Fallen cold and dead,' which anchors the poem's grief and gives it the song-like structure unusual for Whitman's typically unrhymed free verse.

Edgar Allan Poe builds "Annabel Lee" around the repeated phrase 'in this kingdom by the sea' and the name 'Annabel Lee' itself, creating an incantatory, song-like rhythm across the poem's six stanzas.
