Figurative Language

Extended Metaphor

A metaphor that is developed and sustained across multiple lines or an entire poem, rather than appearing in a single phrase.

In Plain English

While a simple metaphor might appear in one line ('life is a journey'), an extended metaphor builds out that comparison in detail over an entire work, adding new facets as the poem progresses. This lets a poet explore a complex or difficult subject indirectly, through the logic of the metaphor's own internal world, rather than stating it outright.

Examples

Walt Whitman's "O Captain! My Captain!" sustains a single extended metaphor across all three stanzas, in which a ship's captain represents Abraham Lincoln, the ship represents the nation, and the safely completed voyage represents the Union's survival through the Civil War — all without ever naming Lincoln directly.
